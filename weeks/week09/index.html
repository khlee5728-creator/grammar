Conditionals & Wish

가정법과 I wish 구문

Units 36 – 39

Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Distinguish between first, second, and third conditionals 1차, 2차, 3차 조건문을 구별할 수 있다
  • Use the correct tense in if-clauses and result clauses if절과 결과절에 올바른 시제를 사용할 수 있다

First Conditional

1차 조건문 — 실현 가능한 미래 상황

Unit 36A (p.72)

Real / Possible Future

Use the first conditional when you think something is a real possibility.

If + present simple, ... will + base form
"If we take the bus, it will be cheaper."
The speaker sees this as a real possibility — they might actually take the bus. 화자는 이것을 실제 가능성으로 봅니다 — 실제로 버스를 탈 수도 있습니다.
"If you study hard, you will pass the exam."

Second Conditional

2차 조건문 — 비현실적 / 상상의 현재 상황

Units 36A–37C (pp.72–75)

Unreal / Imaginary Present

Use the second conditional for situations that are not real now or are unlikely.

If + past simple, ... would + base form
"If we took the bus, it would be cheaper."
They're not going to take the bus — this is imaginary. 실제로 버스를 타지 않을 것입니다 — 이것은 상상입니다.
"I'd call him if I knew his number."
Sarah wants to call Mike but doesn't know his number. Sarah는 Mike에게 전화하고 싶지만 번호를 모릅니다.
"If you were in my position, what would you do?"
Note: Use "were" (not "was") for all subjects in formal English (subjunctive). 격식체에서는 모든 주어에 "were"를 씁니다.

Third Conditional

3차 조건문 — 비현실적 과거 상황

Unit 38A (pp.76–77)

Unreal Past

Use the third conditional to talk about past situations that did not happen.

If + past perfect, ... would have + past participle
"If I'd known you were in the hospital, I would have gone to visit you."
I didn't know, so I didn't visit. I'm imagining a different past. 몰랐기 때문에 방문하지 못했습니다. 다른 과거를 상상하고 있습니다.
"If I'd seen you, I would have said hello."
I didn't see you when you passed me on the street. 길에서 지나칠 때 당신을 보지 못했습니다.

Comparing Conditionals

조건문 비교 — 같은 상황, 세 가지 조건문

Units 36–38

1st Conditional

Real possibility

If + present, will + base

"If we take the bus, it will be cheaper."
We might really take the bus.

2nd Conditional

Imaginary now

If + past, would + base

"If we took the bus, it would be cheaper."
We're not going to take it.

3rd Conditional

Imaginary past

If + past perfect, would have + pp

"If we had taken the bus, it would have been cheaper."
We didn't take it — too late now.

I wish — Present Situations

I wish + 과거형 — 현재 상황에 대한 소망

Unit 37B (pp.74–75)

Wish about the Present

Use I wish + past simple to say you want something to be different now.

I wish + past simple
"I wish I knew Paul's phone number."
I don't know his number now, but I wish I did. 지금 그의 번호를 모르지만, 알았으면 좋겠습니다.
"Do you ever wish you could fly?"
You can't fly — it's an impossible wish about the present. 날 수 없습니다 — 현재에 대한 불가능한 소망입니다.
"I wish there weren't so many people."
It's very crowded here — I want it to be different. 여기가 너무 붐빕니다 — 달랐으면 좋겠습니다.

I wish — Past Regrets

I wish + 과거완료 — 과거에 대한 후회

Unit 38C (pp.76–77)

Regret about the Past

Use I wish + past perfect to express regret about something that already happened.

I wish + past perfect (had + past participle)
"I wish I'd known that Matt was sick. I would have gone to see him."
I didn't know Matt was sick, so I didn't visit him. Now I regret it. Matt가 아픈 줄 몰랐기 때문에 만나러 가지 않았습니다. 지금 후회합니다.
"I feel sick. I wish I hadn't eaten so much."
I ate too much — now I regret it. 너무 많이 먹었습니다 — 지금 후회합니다.

I wish + would

I wish + would — 짜증/요청 표현

Unit 39D (pp.78–79)

Annoyance or Request for Change

Use I wish + would when you are annoyed by something or want someone/something to change.

I wish + subject + would + base form
"I wish it would stop raining."
The rain is annoying and I want it to change. 비가 짜증나고, 그쳤으면 좋겠습니다.
"I wish somebody would answer it."
The phone keeps ringing — somebody answer it! 전화가 계속 울립니다 — 누가 좀 받아 주세요!
"I wish you wouldn't keep interrupting me."
I'm annoyed that you keep interrupting. 계속 끼어들어서 짜증이 납니다.
Note: "I wish I would..." is NOT used. You cannot use "wish + would" about yourself. Say "I wish I could..." instead.

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean does not have conditional tense changes. Korean speakers often use present tense where English requires past tense in unreal conditionals.

한국어에는 가정법 시제 변화가 없어서, 비현실적 가정에서 과거형을 쓰는 것이 어렵습니다.

"If I am rich, I would buy a house."
Using present tense "am" in an unreal conditional is wrong.
"If I were rich, I would buy a house."
The second conditional requires past tense in the if-clause. 2차 조건문에서 if절에는 과거형을 써야 합니다.

More Common Errors

추가적인 흔한 오류

Mixing Conditionals

Don't use "will" in the if-clause of first conditionals.

"If I will have time, I go." → "If I have time, I will go."
Present simple in if-clause; will in result clause. If절에는 현재형, 결과절에는 will.
Wish + Present Tense

Korean speakers may say "I wish I know" instead of "I wish I knew."

"I wish I know the answer." → "I wish I knew the answer."
Wish about the present uses past tense. 현재에 대한 소망은 과거형을 사용합니다.
