Conditionals & Wish

가정법과 I wish 구문

Units 36 – 39

Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Distinguish between first, second, and third conditionals 1차, 2차, 3차 조건문을 구별할 수 있다
  • Use the correct tense in if-clauses and result clauses if절과 결과절에 올바른 시제를 사용할 수 있다
  • Form sentences with "I wish" for present and past situations "I wish" 구문으로 현재와 과거 상황을 표현할 수 있다
  • Avoid common Korean-speaker errors with conditional tenses 조건문 시제에서 한국어 화자의 흔한 실수를 피할 수 있다

First Conditional

1차 조건문 — 실현 가능한 미래 상황

Real / Possible Future

Use the first conditional when you think something is likely to happen in the future.

If + present simple, ... will + base form
"If it rains tomorrow, I'll stay home."
The speaker thinks rain is a real possibility. 화자는 비가 올 가능성이 실제로 있다고 생각합니다.
"If you study hard, you will pass the exam."

Second Conditional

2차 조건문 — 비현실적 / 상상의 현재 상황

Unreal / Imaginary Present

Use the second conditional for situations that are not real now or are unlikely.

If + past simple, ... would + base form
"If I had a million dollars, I would buy a house."
I don't have a million dollars — this is imaginary. 나에게 백만 달러가 없습니다 — 이것은 상상입니다.
"If I were you, I would accept the offer."
Note: We use "were" (not "was") for all subjects in formal English. This is the subjunctive mood. 격식체에서는 모든 주어에 "were"를 씁니다. 이것이 가정법입니다.

Third Conditional

3차 조건문 — 비현실적 과거 상황

Unreal Past

Use the third conditional to talk about past situations that did not happen.

If + past perfect, ... would have + past participle
"If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam."
I didn't study hard enough, so I didn't pass. I'm imagining a different past. 충분히 공부하지 않아서 시험에 떨어졌습니다. 다른 과거를 상상하고 있습니다.
"If she had left earlier, she wouldn't have missed the train."

Comparing Conditionals

조건문 비교

1st Conditional

Real future

If + present, will + base

"If I find your book, I will call you."

2nd Conditional

Unreal present

If + past, would + base

"If I found a wallet, I would return it."

3rd Conditional

Unreal past

If + past perfect, would have + pp

"If I had found it, I would have returned it."

I wish — Present Situations

I wish + 과거형 — 현재 상황에 대한 소망

Wish about the Present

Use I wish + past simple to say you want something to be different now.

I wish + past simple
"I wish I knew the answer."
I don't know the answer now, but I wish I did. 지금 답을 모르지만, 알았으면 좋겠습니다.
"I wish I were taller."
I'm not tall — I want to be different. (Subjunctive "were" used here too.) 키가 크지 않지만, 컸으면 좋겠습니다. (가정법 "were" 사용)

I wish — Past Regrets

I wish + 과거완료 — 과거에 대한 후회

Regret about the Past

Use I wish + past perfect to express regret about something that already happened.

I wish + past perfect (had + past participle)
"I wish I had gone to the party."
I didn't go, and now I regret it. 파티에 가지 않았고, 지금 후회합니다.
"She wishes she had studied medicine."
She didn't study medicine — she regrets this past decision. 그녀는 의학을 공부하지 않았고, 과거의 결정을 후회합니다.

I wish + would

I wish + would — 짜증/요청 표현

Annoyance or Request for Change

Use I wish + would when you are annoyed by something or want someone to change their behavior.

I wish + subject + would + base form
"I wish you would stop making noise."
I'm annoyed — please stop! 짜증이 납니다 — 제발 그만해 주세요!
"I wish it would stop raining."
The rain is annoying and I want it to change. 비가 짜증나고, 그쳤으면 좋겠습니다.
Note: "I wish I would..." is NOT used. You cannot use "wish + would" about yourself. Say "I wish I could..." instead.

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean does not have conditional tense changes. Korean speakers often use present tense where English requires past tense in unreal conditionals.

한국어에는 가정법 시제 변화가 없어서, 비현실적 가정에서 과거형을 쓰는 것이 어렵습니다.

"If I am rich, I would buy a house."
Using present tense "am" in an unreal conditional is wrong.
"If I were rich, I would buy a house."
The second conditional requires past tense in the if-clause. 2차 조건문에서 if절에는 과거형을 써야 합니다.

More Common Errors

추가적인 흔한 오류

Mixing Conditionals

Students sometimes mix first and second conditional forms.

"If I will have time, I go."
Don't use "will" in the if-clause of first conditionals.
"If I have time, I will go."
Present simple in the if-clause; will in the result clause. If절에는 현재형, 결과절에는 will을 씁니다.
Wish + Present Tense

Korean speakers may say "I wish I know" instead of "I wish I knew."

"I wish I know the answer."
"I wish I knew the answer."
Wish about the present uses past tense. 현재에 대한 소망은 과거형을 사용합니다.
