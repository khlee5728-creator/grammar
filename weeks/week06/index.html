Modals 1

조동사 (1)

Units 23 – 29

Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Use can, could, and be able to for ability and permission 능력과 허가에 can, could, be able to를 사용한다
  • Distinguish "could do" from "could have done" "could do"와 "could have done"을 구분한다
  • Use must / can't for logical deduction 논리적 추론에 must / can't를 사용한다
  • Express possibility with may and might 가능성을 may와 might로 표현한다
  • Use may/might have done for past possibility 과거 가능성에 may/might have done을 사용한다

Can

can: 능력, 허가

Can — Ability & Permission (present)

Subject + can + base form
"She can speak three languages."
Present ability. 현재 능력
"Can I use your phone?"
Asking for permission (informal). 허가 요청 (비격식)

Could

could: 과거 능력, 정중한 요청, 가능성

Could — Past ability, Polite requests, Possibility

Subject + could + base form
"I could swim when I was five."
Past ability (general). 과거 능력 (일반적)
"Could you help me, please?"
Polite request. 정중한 요청

Be able to

"할 수 있다" (can이 사용 불가능한 시제에)

"Can" only has present and past forms. For other tenses, use be able to.

"can"은 현재형과 과거형만 있습니다. 다른 시제에서는 "be able to"를 사용합니다.

Future: will be able to
Present perfect: have been able to
Infinitive: to be able to
"I will be able to come to the party."
Future ability. 미래 능력

Could vs Was able to (specific achievement)

특정 상황에서의 성공

"The fire spread quickly, but everyone was able to escape."
Specific situation: they managed to escape. 특정 상황에서 탈출에 성공함.
"Everyone could escape."
For specific past achievements, use "was/were able to" (not "could"). 특정 과거 성과에는 "was able to"를 사용합니다.

Could (do) vs Could have (done)

could do vs could have done 비교

could do

Present/future possibility or ability.

현재/미래의 가능성 또는 능력

"We could go to the cinema tonight."
It's a possibility now.

could have done

Past possibility that didn't happen.

일어나지 않은 과거의 가능성

"We could have gone to the cinema, but we stayed home."
It was possible, but we didn't.
"You could have told me earlier!"
You had the chance but you didn't. (Mild criticism) 기회가 있었는데 하지 않음 (가벼운 비판)

Must / Can't for Deduction

추론의 must / can't

Making logical deductions about the present

현재 상황에 대한 논리적 추론

must be = I'm sure it IS true
can't be = I'm sure it ISN'T true
"He's been working all day. He must be tired."
I'm fairly certain (based on evidence). 증거에 근거해 상당히 확신.
"She can't be at home. Her car isn't there."
I'm sure it's NOT true (evidence: no car). 사실이 아님을 확신 (증거: 차 없음).
Note: This is NOT about obligation. "must be" here means "I'm sure / I conclude that..." 여기서 must는 의무가 아닌 추론/확신을 나타냅니다.

Must have / Can't have (Past Deduction)

과거에 대한 추론

must have + V3 = I'm sure it happened
can't have + V3 = I'm sure it didn't happen
"She knew about the party. Someone must have told her."
I conclude that someone told her. 누군가가 그녀에게 말했음이 틀림없다.
"He can't have seen us. He wasn't wearing his glasses."
I'm sure he didn't see us. 그가 우리를 보지 못했음이 확실하다.

May / Might

가능성의 may / might

Expressing possibility

may / might + base form = It's possible that...

may and might are very similar. "Might" is slightly less certain.

may와 might는 매우 비슷합니다. might가 약간 덜 확실합니다.

"She might come to the party."
It's possible (but uncertain). 가능하지만 불확실함.
"It may rain later."
Possible. 가능함.
"I might not go to the gym today."
It's possible I won't go. 안 갈 수도 있음.

May / Might have done

과거 가능성: may/might have + 과거분사

may / might have + past participle

For something that possibly happened in the past (but we're not sure).

과거에 일어났을 수도 있는 일 (확실하지 않음).

"He might have forgotten about our meeting."
It's possible that he forgot. 그가 잊었을 가능성이 있음.
"She may have left already."
Perhaps she left. 아마 그녀가 이미 떠났을 수도 있음.

Certainty Scale

확신도 척도

must be — 95% sure (almost certain) 거의 확실함
will (probably) — 85% sure 아마 그럴 것
may — 50% possible ~할 수도 있음
might / could — 30% possible ~할 수도 있음 (덜 확실)
can't be — 95% sure it's NOT true 거의 확실히 아님

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ Confusing "can" and "could" in polite requests

Korean uses the same marker for both levels of politeness. In English, "could" is more polite than "can" for requests.

한국어에서는 존댓말로 정중함을 표현하지만, 영어에서는 could가 can보다 더 정중합니다.

"Can you pass me the salt?" (to a professor)
"Could you pass me the salt, please?"
More polite for formal situations. 격식 있는 상황에서 더 정중함.
⚠ Using "can" where "be able to" is needed

"can"이 사용 불가능한 시제에서 "be able to"를 써야 합니다.

"I want to can speak English fluently."
"I want to be able to speak English fluently."
After "to," use "be able to" (not "can"). "to" 뒤에는 "be able to"를 사용합니다.
