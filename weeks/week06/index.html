Modals 1

조동사 (1)

Units 23 – 29

Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Use "will be doing" and "will have done" for future continuous & perfect 미래진행형과 미래완료형을 사용한다 (Unit 23)
  • Use present tense (not future) in when/if clauses when/if 절에서 현재시제를 사용한다 (Unit 24)
  • Use can, could, and be able to for ability and permission 능력과 허가에 can, could, be able to를 사용한다 (Units 25–26)
  • Distinguish "could do" from "could have done" "could do"와 "could have done"을 구분한다 (Unit 26)
  • Use must / can't for logical deduction 논리적 추론에 must / can't를 사용한다 (Unit 27)
  • Express possibility with may and might; use "might as well" 가능성을 may/might로 표현하고, might as well을 사용한다 (Units 28–29)

will be doing and will have done Unit 23

📖 GIU Unit 23A–D, p.46-47

will be doing = in the middle of an action at a future time
will have done = completed before a future time
"Don't phone between 7 and 8. We'll be eating."
"This time next week I'll be lying on the beach."

will have done

"Emily always leaves at 8:30. She won't be at home at 9:00 — she'll have gone to work."

when I do and when I've done Unit 24A

when/if + 현재시제 (미래 아님)

📖 GIU Unit 24A, p.48-49

Time clauses with when / if — Use present, NOT future

📖 "The time is future, but Amy says: when I arrive (not when I will arrive)." (Unit 24A)

when / if 절에서는 미래형이 아닌 현재형을 사용합니다.

when/if + present simple, ... will ...
when + present perfect, ... will ...
"I'll call you again when I arrive."
NOT "when I will arrive." "when I will arrive"가 아님.
"Don't forget to lock the door when you'll go out."
"Don't forget to lock the door when you go out."

when I do and when I've done / if and when Unit 24B–C

when + 현재완료 / if + 현재시제

📖 GIU Unit 24B–C, p.48-49

when + present perfect (completed before main action)

when + 현재완료 (주된 동작 전에 완료)

"When I've called Kate, we can go out."
After I've finished calling Kate. Kate에게 전화를 마친 후에.
"I'll be angry if it happens again."
"if" follows the same rule: present tense, not future. if도 같은 규칙: 현재시제.

can, could, and (be) able to Unit 25A

📖 GIU Unit 25A, p.50-51

Can — Ability & Permission

📖 "We use can to say that something is possible or allowed, or that somebody has the ability to do something." (Unit 25A)

Subject + can + base form
"We can see the lake from our hotel."
"Can you speak any foreign languages?"
"I can't come to the party on Friday."

could (do) and could have (done) Unit 26A–B

📖 GIU Unit 26A–B, p.52-53

Could — Suggestions, Possibility, Past ability

📖 "We also use could for possible actions now or in the future, especially to make suggestions." (Unit 26A)

Subject + could + base form
"What should we do tonight?" "We could go to the movies."
"When you go to New York next month, you could stay with Sarah."
"I'm so tired. I could sleep for a week."

can, could, and (be) able to Unit 25B

"할 수 있다" (can이 사용 불가능한 시제에)

📖 GIU Unit 25B, p.50-51

"Can" only has present and past forms. For other tenses, use be able to.

"can"은 현재형과 과거형만 있습니다. 다른 시제에서는 "be able to"를 사용합니다.

Future: will be able to / Present perfect: have been able to / Infinitive: to be able to
"I haven't been able to sleep lately."
Present perfect — "can" is impossible here. 현재완료 — 여기서 can은 사용 불가.

could and was able to Unit 25D

could vs was able to (특정 상황에서의 성공)

📖 GIU Unit 25D, p.50-51

"The fire spread quickly, but everybody was able to escape."
Specific situation: they managed to escape. 특정 상황에서 탈출에 성공함.
"Everybody could escape."
For specific past achievements, use "was/were able to" (not "could"). 특정 과거 성과에는 "was able to"를 사용.
"Jack was an excellent tennis player. He could beat anybody."
General past ability (not specific) — "could" is OK. 일반적 과거 능력 — could 가능.

could (do) and could have (done) Unit 26C–D

could do vs could have done 비교

📖 GIU Unit 26C–D, p.52-53

could do (now/future)

Present/future possibility. 현재/미래 가능성

"I'm so tired. I could sleep for a week."

could have done (past)

Past possibility that didn't happen. 일어나지 않은 과거 가능성

"I was so tired I could have slept for a week."
"Why did you stay at a hotel? You could have stayed with me."
You had the option but didn't use it. 선택지가 있었지만 사용하지 않음.

must and can't Unit 27A

📖 GIU Unit 27A, p.54-55

Logical deductions about the present

📖 "We use must to say that we feel sure something is true." (Unit 27A)

must be = I'm sure it IS true
can't be = I'm sure it ISN'T true
"You've been traveling all day. You must be tired."
"He just had lunch. He can't be hungry."
Note: This is NOT about obligation. "must be" here means "I'm sure / I conclude that..."

must (not) have done Unit 27B

과거에 대한 추론

📖 GIU Unit 27B, p.54-55

must have + V3 = I'm sure it happened
can't have + V3 = I'm sure it didn't happen
"I lost one of my gloves. I must have dropped it somewhere."
I conclude that I dropped it. 어딘가에서 떨어뜨린 것이 틀림없다.
"There's nobody home. They must have gone out."
I'm sure they went out. 그들이 외출한 것이 틀림없다.

may and might 1 Unit 28A

📖 GIU Unit 28A, p.56-57

Expressing possibility

📖 "We use may or might to say that something is possible." (Unit 28A)

may / might + base form = It's possible that...

may and might are very similar in meaning.

"Where's Ben?" "He may be in his office." / "He might be having lunch."
"She may not know." / "She might not know."
"could" is similar: "It could be true." / "You could have left your phone at work."

may/might have done Unit 28B

과거 가능성: may/might have + 과거분사

📖 GIU Unit 28B, p.56-57

may / might have + past participle

For something that possibly happened in the past (but we're not sure).

과거에 일어났을 수도 있는 일 (확실하지 않음).

"She may have been asleep."
It's possible she was asleep. 그녀가 자고 있었을 수도 있음.
"You might have left it at work."
Perhaps you left it there. 아마 직장에 두고 왔을 수도 있음.

may and might 2 Unit 29A–B

may/might: 미래 가능성

📖 GIU Unit 29A–B, p.58-59

Future possibility

📖 "We use may and might to talk about possible actions or events in the future." (Unit 29A)

may / might + base form = possible future action
"I may go to Hawaii." / "It might rain later."
Future possibility (not decided yet). 미래 가능성 (아직 미정).

might (not may) for unreal situations

비현실적 상황에서는 might를 사용 (may 아님)

"If they paid me better, I might work harder."
Unreal/hypothetical — use "might" (not "may"). 비현실적/가정 — might 사용.

may and might 2: might as well Unit 29C–D

might as well / may/might be -ing

📖 GIU Unit 29C–D, p.58-59

"might as well" = no better alternative

might as well = 더 나은 대안이 없음 (~하는 게 낫다)

"We might as well walk."
There's no better alternative. 더 나은 대안이 없음.

may/might be -ing (possible action in progress)

"Don't phone at 8:30. I might be watching football on TV."
Possible action in progress at a future time. 미래 시점에 진행 중일 수 있는 동작.

Certainty Scale

확신도 척도

must be — 95% sure (almost certain) 거의 확실함
will (probably) — 85% sure 아마 그럴 것
may — 50% possible ~할 수도 있음
might / could — 30% possible ~할 수도 있음 (덜 확실)
can't be — 95% sure it's NOT true 거의 확실히 아님

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ Confusing "can" and "could" in polite requests

영어에서는 could가 can보다 더 정중합니다.

"Can you pass me the salt?" (to a professor) → "Could you pass me the salt, please?"
⚠ Using "can" where "be able to" is needed

"can"이 사용 불가능한 시제에서 "be able to"를 써야 합니다.

"I want to can speak English fluently."
"I want to be able to speak English fluently."
After "to," use "be able to" (not "can"). "to" 뒤에는 "be able to"를 사용.

Week 6 Summary

  • will be doing / will have done: future continuous & perfect (U23)
  • when/if + present: time clauses use present, not future (U24)
  • can / be able to: present ability; "be able to" for other tenses (U25)
  • could do / could have done: possibility now vs unrealized past (U26)
  • must be / can't be: deduction — sure / sure not (U27)
  • may / might: possibility; may/might have done for past (U28)
  • may/might 2: "might" for unreal; "might as well" (U29)
