Present Perfect vs Simple Past

현재완료 vs 단순과거: 핵심 차이

Present Perfect

Connection to now. 현재와 연결.

“Tom has lost his key.”
He can’t get in NOW. 지금 집에 못 들어간다.

Simple Past

Finished time. 끝난 시간.

“Tom lost his key yesterday.”
He couldn’t get in. 어제 집에 못 들어갔다.
“I’ve done a lot of work today.” (not finished)  vs  “I did a lot of work yesterday.” (finished)

📘 GIU Unit 13, p.26-27

Time Signals

시간 신호 단어

Present Perfect

ever / never
just / already / yet
so far / up to now
recently / lately
today / this week / this year
for / since

Simple Past

yesterday
last week / last month / last year
ago (two days ago)
in 2019 / in July
when I was young
at that time / then
“It hasn’t rained this week.”
This week is not finished → present perfect. 이번 주는 아직 끝나지 않았다.
“It didn’t rain last week.”
Last week is finished → simple past. 지난주는 끝난 시간이다.

📘 GIU Unit 13, p.26-27

Present Perfect vs Past: Context Matters

맥락에 따라 달라지는 시제

“Have you seen Anna this morning?” (It is still morning.)
This morning is not finished → present perfect. 아직 아침이므로 현재완료.
“Did you see Anna this morning?” (It is now afternoon.)
This morning is finished → simple past. 이미 오후이므로 아침은 끝남 → 단순과거.
“Jack lives in Los Angeles. He has lived there for seven years.”
He still lives there → present perfect. 아직 거기 살고 있으므로 현재완료.
“Jack lived in New York for ten years. Now he lives in Los Angeles.”
He doesn’t live in New York anymore → simple past. 더 이상 뉴욕에 살지 않으므로 단순과거.

📘 GIU Unit 13, p.26-27

Past Perfect: Formation

과거완료: 형태

The “past of the past”

“과거의 과거” — 과거의 한 시점보다 더 먼저 일어난 일

Subject + had + past participle (V3)

Use the past perfect for an action that happened before another past action.

📘 GIU Unit 14, p.28-29

Past Perfect on the Timeline

타임라인으로 보는 과거완료

“When Sarah arrived at the party, Nick had already gone home.”
Nick left (earlier) → Sarah arrived (later). Both in the past.
Nick이 먼저 떠남 → Sarah가 나중에 도착. 둘 다 과거이지만 순서가 다름.
“When we got home last night, we found that somebody had broken into the apartment.”
The break-in happened before we got home. 침입이 먼저 일어남 → 우리가 나중에 도착.
“Lauren didn’t come to the movies with us. She’d already seen the movie.”
Seeing the movie happened first → past perfect. 영화를 이미 봤기 때문에 같이 가지 않았다.

📘 GIU Unit 14, p.28-29

Past Perfect vs Present Perfect

과거완료와 현재완료 비교

Present Perfect

“I’ve seen her before, but I can’t remember where.”
Past → present (can’t remember now). 과거 → 현재 (지금 기억이 안 남).

Past Perfect

“I’d seen her before, but I couldn’t remember where.”
Past → earlier past (couldn’t remember then). 과거 → 더 이전의 과거 (그때 기억이 안 났음).
“The people sitting next to me on the plane were nervous. They hadn’t flown before.”
They had no flying experience before that flight. 그 비행 전에 비행기를 타본 적이 없었다.

📘 GIU Unit 14, p.28-29

Past Perfect Continuous

과거완료 진행형

Subject + had been + verb-ing

Emphasizes the duration of an activity that was in progress before another past event.

다른 과거 사건 이전에 진행 중이던 활동의 기간을 강조합니다.

“It had been raining.” (The ground was wet.)
Evidence of a recently completed activity. 비가 오고 있었다 (땅이 젖어 있었다).
“He’d been working hard all day.” (Tom was tired.)
The long activity explains why he was tired. 하루 종일 열심히 일해서 Tom이 피곤했다.

📘 GIU Unit 15, p.30-31

Past Perfect Continuous: More Examples

과거완료 진행형: 추가 예문

“We’d been playing tennis for about half an hour when it started to rain heavily.”
Activity in progress + duration before another past event. 비가 많이 오기 시작하기 전에 약 30분 동안 테니스를 치고 있었다.

Present Perfect Continuous

“I’ve been waiting for 20 minutes.”
Still waiting now. 지금도 기다리는 중.

Past Perfect Continuous

“I’d been waiting for 20 minutes when the bus came.”
Waiting ended when the bus came. 버스가 왔을 때 20분간 기다리고 있었다.

📘 GIU Unit 15, p.30-31

Have and Have Got

have와 have got

have and have got can both express possession, relationships, and characteristics.

have와 have got은 둘 다 소유, 관계, 특성을 나타낼 수 있습니다.

have

“They have a new car.”
“Do you have any questions?”

have got

“They’ve got a new car.”
“Have you got any questions?”
⚠ “have” expressions — do NOT use “have got”

have breakfast, have a party, have an accident, have a good time, have trouble, have a look

이런 표현에서는 have got을 쓸 수 없습니다.

“We’re enjoying our vacation. We’re having a great time.”
“have a great time” = an action/experience, not possession. Can use continuous.
“have a great time” = 소유가 아닌 경험/행위. 진행형 사용 가능.

📘 GIU Unit 16, p.32-33

Used to (do)

“예전에는 ~했다” (과거 습관/상태)

Subject + used to + base form (V1)

For past habits or states that are no longer true.

더 이상 사실이 아닌 과거의 습관이나 상태를 나타냅니다.

“She used to travel a lot.”
She traveled often in the past, but doesn’t anymore. 과거에 많이 여행했지만 지금은 아니다.
“I used to play tennis a lot, but I don’t play very much now.”
Past habit, no longer true. 과거 습관, 지금은 아님.
“This building is now a furniture store. It used to be a movie theater.”
Past state, no longer exists. 과거 상태, 더 이상 존재하지 않음.

📘 GIU Unit 17, p.34-35

Used to: Questions & Comparison

used to: 의문문 & 비교

“Did you use to eat a lot of candy when you were a child?”
In questions and negatives: did … use to (no “d”). 의문문/부정문: did … use to (“d” 없음).

used to + V1

“I used to watch TV a lot.”
Past habit. 과거 습관.

past continuous

“I was watching TV when Rob called.”
Action in progress at a specific past moment. 특정 과거 시점에 진행 중이던 동작.

📘 GIU Unit 17, p.34-35

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ Overusing Past Perfect

Korean speakers sometimes use past perfect when simple past is sufficient. Only use past perfect when the time sequence matters.

과거완료는 시간 순서가 중요할 때만 사용합니다. 단순히 과거를 말할 때는 단순과거를 사용합니다.

“I had gone to school and had studied hard.”
No need for past perfect here; no earlier-later relationship.
“I went to school and studied hard.”
⚠ Confusing “used to” with “be used to”

“used to + V1” (과거 습관)와 “be used to + V-ing” (익숙하다)를 혼동하지 마세요.

“I used to wake up early.” (= I am accustomed to it)
This means “I did it in the past (but not now),” NOT “I’m accustomed to it.”
“I am used to waking up early.” (= I am accustomed to it)
be used to + V-ing = accustomed to. be used to + V-ing = ~에 익숙하다

Week 4 Summary

4주차 요약

  • Present Perfect: connection to now, time unspecified 현재완료: 현재와 연결, 시간 불특정
  • Simple Past: finished time, specific past time 단순과거: 끝난 시간, 특정 과거 시점
  • Past Perfect (had + V3): the “past of the past” 과거완료: 과거의 과거
  • Past Perfect Continuous (had been + V-ing): duration before a past event 과거완료 진행형: 과거 사건 이전의 지속
  • have / have got: both express possession; use “have” (not “have got”) for actions have / have got: 둘 다 소유 표현; 행위 표현에는 have만 사용
  • used to + V1: past habits/states no longer true used to: 더 이상 사실이 아닌 과거 습관/상태
  • Don’t confuse used to (past habit) with be used to (accustomed to) used to (과거 습관)와 be used to (익숙하다)를 혼동하지 마세요
