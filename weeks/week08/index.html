Quick Review: Present & Past

Units 1–6 • 현재 & 과거 시제

Present Continuous

am/is/are + V-ing

Happening now or around now

The water is boiling. Can you turn it off?

Simple Present

V / V-s (he/she/it)

Facts, habits, repeated actions

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

State Verbs

Not usually used in continuous form:

I am knowing → I know

know, like, want, need, prefer, believe, belong, mean...

Past Simple vs Past Continuous

V-ed / was-were + V-ing
I was walking home when it started to rain.

📖 GIU Units 1–6, p.2-13

Briefly review each tense. Ask students to give their own examples. Remind them about state verbs - this is a common mistake area. Past continuous = background action; past simple = interrupting action.

Quick Review: Present Perfect

Units 7–12 • 현재완료

Present Perfect Simple

have/has + past participle (V3)

Past action with present result or relevance

I have lost my keys. (= I can't find them now)

PP vs Simple Past

PP: No specific time / still relevant now

Past: Specific time / finished

I 've been to Paris. (= experience)
I went to Paris last year.

for vs since

for + period of time (for 3 years, for a long time)

since + point in time (since 2020, since Monday)

She has lived here for 5 years / since 2021.

Present Perfect Continuous

have/has been + V-ing

Emphasis on duration or activity itself

It has been raining all day.

📖 GIU Units 7–12, p.14-25

Present perfect is the most challenging tense for Korean speakers because Korean does not have a direct equivalent. Emphasize the connection to NOW. The key question: "Does this matter NOW?" If yes, present perfect.

Quick Review: Past Perfect & Used to

Units 13–17 • 과거완료 & used to

Past Perfect

had + past participle (V3)

An action that happened before another past action 과거의 과거: 과거의 한 시점보다 더 이전에 일어난 일

When I arrived, the party had already started.
She wasn't hungry because she had eaten lunch.

used to + base form

used to + V (base form)

Past states or habits that are no longer true

I used to live in Busan, but now I live in Seoul.

더 이상 사실이 아닌 과거의 상태나 습관

Common Confusion

Do not confuse:

used to (past habit)
be used to + V-ing (= be accustomed to)

I am used to getting up early. (= It's normal for me)

📖 GIU Units 13–17, p.26-35

Past perfect: draw a simple timeline on the board to illustrate. "used to" vs "be used to" is a classic exam question. Ask students: "What did you use to do when you were a child?"

Quick Review: Future

Units 18–24 • 미래 시제

will

will + V (base form)

Decisions at the moment, predictions, promises, offers

"I'm cold." — "I'll close the window."

순간 결정 / 예측 / 약속 / 제안

going to

am/is/are going to + V

Plans already decided, predictions with evidence

Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.

이미 결정된 계획 / 증거 기반 예측

Present Continuous for Future

am/is/are + V-ing (+ future time)

Fixed arrangements / appointments

I 'm meeting John at 3 o'clock tomorrow.

확정된 약속이나 일정

Future Continuous / Perfect

will be + V-ing / will have + V3

Continuous: action in progress at a future time

Perfect: action completed before a future time

This time tomorrow, I will be flying to Tokyo.

📖 GIU Units 18–24, p.36-49

The will vs going to distinction is a favourite exam question. Key: will = spontaneous decision; going to = already planned. Present continuous for future = specific arrangement with another person/place.

Quick Review: Modals

Units 23–35 • 조동사

can / could / be able to

can = present ability

could = past general ability

was able to = specific past achievement

I could swim when I was five.

must / can't (deduction)

must = I'm sure it's true (positive)

can't = I'm sure it's not true (negative)

You've been working all day. You must be tired.

may / might (possibility)

may / might = it's possible (maybe)

might = slightly less certain than may

She might be at home, or she might be at work.

must vs have to / should

must = personal obligation or rule

have to = external obligation

don't have to = not necessary

should / had better = advice

You should see a doctor.

📖 GIU Units 25–35, p.50-71

Modals cover a large range of units. Focus on the key distinctions: 1. must (deduction) vs must (obligation) 2. don't have to (no obligation) vs mustn't (prohibition) 3. should (advice) vs had better (stronger advice with warning)

Common Mistakes (1)

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수 (1–5)

Top 10 Mistakes Korean Speakers Make (Units 1–35)
# Incorrect Correct Rule
1 I am knowing the answer. I know the answer. State verbs: no continuous
2 I have been to Paris last year. I went to Paris last year. Specific past time = simple past
3 I live here since 2020. I have lived here since 2020. "since" requires present perfect
4 When I arrived, the movie already started. ...the movie had already started. Before another past = past perfect
5 I will meet John tomorrow at 3. (arranged) I am meeting John tomorrow at 3. Fixed arrangement = present continuous
Go through each mistake. Ask students if they can explain WHY each correction is correct. Mistakes 2 and 3 are the most common on exams.

Common Mistakes (2)

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수 (6–10)

Top 10 Mistakes Korean Speakers Make (Units 1–35)
# Incorrect Correct Rule
6 "It's cold." "I'm going to close the window." "It's cold." "I'll close the window." Spontaneous decision = will
7 You must not come if you don't want to. You don't have to come... mustn't = prohibited; don't have to = optional
8 She can play piano when she was young. She could play piano when... Past ability = could (not can)
9 I use to play soccer. I used to play soccer. "used to" (with -d), not "use to"
10 I have gone to Japan three times. I have been to Japan three times. Experience = have been to (not gone to)
Continue going through each mistake. Mistake 7 (mustn't vs don't have to) is one of the most common on exams. Great opportunity for class discussion.

Study Tips for the Exam

시험 대비 학습 팁

1. Review the Rules

Understand when and why to use each form, not just the form itself.

형태뿐 아니라 언제, 왜 쓰는지를 이해하세요.

2. Focus on Differences

Practice distinguishing: will vs going to, present perfect vs past simple, must vs have to.

비슷한 형태의 차이점에 집중하세요.

3. Read the Context

Look for time markers, signal words, and the meaning of the whole sentence.

시간 표현, 신호 단어, 문장 전체 의미를 파악하세요.

4. Practice with Exercises

Redo the textbook exercises, especially ones you got wrong before.

틀렸던 문제를 다시 풀어보는 것이 효과적입니다.

Good luck on the exam!

자신감을 갖고 시험 보세요! 행운을 빕니다!

End on a positive note. Remind students of office hours if they need extra help. Suggest forming study groups to review together. Remind them to get enough sleep before the exam.
