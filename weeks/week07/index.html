Must vs Have to

must vs have to: 의무의 차이

📘 GIU Unit 30, p.60-61

must (internal)

The speaker feels it is necessary. Personal feeling or decision.

화자가 필요하다고 느낌. 개인적 판단.

have to (external)

External rule, law, or requirement. Not the speaker's choice.

외부 규칙, 법, 요구사항. 화자의 선택이 아님.

Must vs Have to: Examples

must vs have to: 예문

📘 GIU Unit 30, p.60-61

must (internal)

"You must stop working all the time. Get out and have some fun!"
Personal advice — the speaker feels strongly. 개인적 조언 — 화자가 강하게 느낌.

have to (external)

"You can't turn right here. You have to turn left."
External rule (traffic regulation). 외부 규칙 (교통 규정).

Must not vs Don't have to

부정형: 금지 vs 불필요

📘 GIU Unit 30, p.60-61

The negatives are VERY different!

부정형의 의미가 매우 다릅니다!

must not (mustn't) = It is forbidden (prohibited)
don't have to = It is not necessary (optional)
"You must keep this a secret. You must not tell anyone."
It's prohibited! Don't tell! 금지! 말하면 안 됨.
"You don't have to come with me. I can go alone."
It's not necessary (but you can if you want). 필요 없음 (하지만 원하면 해도 됨).
⚠ Korean speakers often confuse these!

"~하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "~할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동하지 마세요!

Should

should: 조언, 의견, 권고

📘 GIU Unit 31, p.62-63

Subject + should + base form

Use should for advice, opinions, and recommendations. Weaker than "must."

조언, 의견, 권고에 사용합니다. "must"보다 약합니다.

"You look tired. You should go to bed."
Advice. 조언
"The government should do more to improve schools."
Opinion. 의견
"The man on the motorcycle should be wearing a helmet."
should + be + -ing: something is not right now. should + be + -ing: 지금 옳지 않은 상황.

Should have done

"~했어야 했는데" (과거에 대한 후회/비판)

📘 GIU Unit 31, p.62-63

Subject + should have + past participle

Something was the right thing to do, but you didn't do it.

올바른 행동이었지만 하지 않았음을 나타냅니다.

"You missed a great party last night. You should have come. Why didn't you?"
Regret: You didn't come, but it was a great party. 후회: 오지 않았지만 좋은 파티였다.
"I'm feeling sick. I shouldn't have eaten so much."
Regret: I ate too much and now I feel bad. 후회: 너무 많이 먹어서 기분이 안 좋다.
"She shouldn't have been listening to our conversation. It was private."
Criticism: she did something wrong. 비판: 그녀는 잘못한 것이다.

Subjunctive: suggest / recommend that...

접속법: suggest/recommend + that + 주어 + 동사원형

📘 GIU Unit 32, p.64-65

Special grammar after suggest, recommend, insist, demand, propose

suggest / recommend + that + subject + base form

The verb after "that" uses the base form (no -s, no past tense).

"that" 뒤의 동사는 원형을 사용합니다 (-s 없음, 과거형 아님).

"Liz suggested that Mary buy some nice clothes."
NOT "buys" or "bought" — use the base form. "buys"나 "bought"가 아닌 원형 "buy"를 사용.
"The doctor recommended that I rest for a few days."
"Liz suggested that Mary buys some nice clothes." — Incorrect in formal English.

Had better & It's time...

had better: 강한 조언 / 경고 & It's time...

📘 GIU Unit 33, p.66-67

Subject + had better ('d better) + base form

Stronger than "should." Implies a warning — something bad will happen if you don't follow the advice.

"should"보다 강합니다. 경고의 의미 — 조언을 따르지 않으면 나쁜 일이 생깁니다.

"I have to meet Amy in ten minutes. I'd better go now or I'll be late."
Warning: bad consequence if you don't. 경고: 안 하면 나쁜 결과.
"You don't look very well. You'd better not go out tonight."
Negative: had better not + base form. 부정형: had better not + 동사원형.
Note: "had better" is for specific situations, not general advice. For general advice, use "should." "had better"는 특정 상황에 대한 것입니다. 일반적인 조언에는 "should"를 사용합니다.

Would (1)

would: 가정, 과거 습관

📘 GIU Unit 34, p.68-69

1. Imaginary / hypothetical situations

would + base form = imaginary present/future
"It would be nice to buy a new car, but we can't afford it."
Imaginary — not real. 상상 — 현실이 아님.

2. Past habits (repeated actions)

Similar to "used to" but ONLY for repeated actions (not states).

"used to"와 비슷하지만 반복적 행동에만 사용 (상태에는 사용 불가).

"When we were children, we would all get up early and go for a swim."
Repeated past action. 반복적인 과거 행동.

Would (2)

would: 과거 습관, 거절

📘 GIU Unit 34, p.68-69

3. Past refusal (wouldn't)

wouldn't = refused to do something in the past.

wouldn't = 과거에 ~하기를 거부했다.

"Whenever Tony was angry, he would walk out of the room."
Past repeated habit. 과거 반복 습관.
"I tried to warn him, but he wouldn't listen to me."
Past refusal: he refused to listen. 과거 거절: 듣기를 거부했다.

Would: Polite Expressions

would: 정중한 표현

📘 GIU Unit 34 & 35, p.68-71

Polite requests, offers, and preferences

"Would you like some coffee?"
Polite offer. 정중한 제안.
"Would you like to eat with us tonight?"
Polite invitation. 정중한 초대.
"I'd like some information about hotels, please."
Polite request (I'd like = I would like). 정중한 요청 (I'd like = I would like).

Can / Could / Would you...? (1)

요청의 정중함 단계

📘 GIU Unit 35, p.70-71

Politeness levels for requests

Can you...? → informal request
Could you...? → more polite
Would you mind...? → most polite

정중함: Can < Could < Would you mind

"Can you wait a minute, please?"
Informal request. 비격식 요청.
"Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the bus station?"
More polite request (to a stranger). 더 정중한 요청 (낯선 사람에게).

Can / Could / Would you...? (2)

제안과 허가

📘 GIU Unit 35, p.70-71

Offers & Permission

Can I...? = asking permission / offering help
Could I...? = more polite permission
"Can I help you?" / "Can I get you some coffee?"
Offering help. 도움 제안.
"Could I use your phone charger?"
Polite permission request. 정중한 허가 요청.

Obligation & Advice Scale

의무 & 조언 강도 척도

must / have to — Strong obligation 강한 의무
had better — Strong advice / warning 강한 조언 / 경고
should / ought to — Advice / recommendation 조언 / 권고
could / might — Suggestion (weaker) 제안 (더 약함)
don't have to — Not necessary 불필요 (해도 되고 안 해도 됨)
must not — Prohibited 금지

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers (1)

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수 (1)

⚠ Mixing up must / have to

Korean "~해야 한다" doesn't distinguish between internal and external obligation. In English, the difference matters.

한국어 "~해야 한다"는 내적/외적 의무를 구분하지 않지만, 영어에서는 차이가 있습니다.

⚠ Overusing "should" where "would" is appropriate

"would"가 적절한 곳에 "should"를 과다 사용하는 실수.

"When I was a child, I should play outside every day."
"When I was a child, I would play outside every day."
Past habit = "would" or "used to," not "should." 과거 습관 = "would" 또는 "used to"

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers (2)

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수 (2)

⚠ Confusing mustn't and don't have to

"하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동.

"You mustn't bring food. It's optional."
"You don't have to bring food. It's optional."
"Optional" = not necessary = don't have to. "선택사항" = 필요 없음 = don't have to
