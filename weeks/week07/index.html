have to — Present & Past Unit 30A ①

have to: 해야 한다 (의무/필요) — 현재와 과거

📖 GIU Unit 30A, p.60-61

A have to do something = it is necessary to do it

Subject + have/has to + base verb
PRESENT
"You have to turn left at the traffic lights."
"He has to work late this evening."
PAST — had to
"Last week Emily broke her arm and had to go to the hospital."

have to — Questions, Future, Possibility Unit 30A ②

질문·부정문은 do/does/did, 미래·가능성 표현

📖 GIU Unit 30A, p.60-61

QUESTIONS / NEGATIVES — do / does / did
"What do I have to do to get a driver's license?"
"Karen doesn't have to work on Saturdays."
"Did you have to wait a long time?"
FUTURE — 'll have to / 'm going to have to
"I'll have to buy a new computer." = "I'm going to have to buy a new computer."
POSSIBILITY — might / may have to
"We might have to change our plans." (= it's possible we'll have to)

must Unit 30

must: ~해야 한다 (강한 의무)

📖 GIU Unit 30B, p.60-61

B must is similar to have to

must and have to are often similar, but have to is more common. must와 have to는 비슷하지만, have to가 더 자주 쓰임.

GENERAL USE
"The government must do something about it."
"We must reduce pollution to help future generations."
WRITTEN RULES & INSTRUCTIONS
"Applications for the job must be received by May 18."
"Seat belts must be worn."
⚠ Past — use had to (not must)
"I went to the meeting yesterday, but I had to leave early." 과거는 항상 had to.

mustn't, don't have to, have got to Unit 30

부정형 (금지 vs 불필요) & 대체 표현

📖 GIU Unit 30C–D, p.60-61

C must not and don't have to are completely different

must not (mustn't) — 금지

It is forbidden. 하면 안 된다.

"You must not tell anyone. Keep it secret."

don't have to — 불필요

It is not necessary. 할 필요 없다.

"You don't have to come with me. I can go alone."
⚠ Korean speakers often confuse these!

"~하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "~할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동하지 마세요!

D have got to = have to (informal)

"I've got to work tomorrow." = "I have to work tomorrow."
"He's got to visit his aunt tonight." = "He has to visit his aunt tonight."

should — Advice & Asking Unit 31A–B

조언·의견 주기, 조언·제안 구하기

📖 GIU Unit 31A–B, p.62-63

A should = it's a good idea (giving advice/opinion)

Subject + should + base form
"You look tired. You should go to bed." (Advice)
"The government should do more to improve schools." (Opinion)

B Should I ...? / Should we ...? (asking for a suggestion)

Use Should I/we ...? to ask if something is OK or to ask for a suggestion. ~해도 될까? / ~할까? (제안·허락 구하기)

"Are you cold? Should I close the window?"
"I don't have any money. What should I do?"   "Where should we have lunch?"

should — Expectation Unit 31C

기대와 다를 때 / 일어날 것으로 예상할 때

📖 GIU Unit 31C, p.62-63

C should = expected to happen (but it isn't / it will)

should expresses what we expect — either what we think is true now, or what we think will happen. "당연히 ~해야 하는데(안 함)" 또는 "~할 것으로 예상된다".

NOT RIGHT — 기대와 다른 현재
"Where's Hannah? She should be here by now." (= I expect her to be here)
EXPECTATION — 앞으로 예상
"Helen has been studying hard, so she should pass the exam."

should have done Unit 31

"~했어야 했는데" (과거에 대한 후회/비판)

📖 GIU Unit 31D, p.62-63

D should have done = regret

Subject + should have + past participle

📖 "You should have done something = you didn't do it, but it would have been the right thing to do." (Unit 31D)

The right thing to do, but you didn't do it.

"You missed a great party. You should have come." 후회
"I'm feeling sick. I shouldn't have eaten so much." 후회
"She shouldn't have been listening to our conversation." 비판

Subjunctive (I suggest you do) Unit 32A–B

접속법: 주어·시제와 무관하게 동사원형

📖 GIU Unit 32A–B, p.64-65

A Subjunctive = base form (원형)

... + that + subject + base form (buy / be / have …)
"Liz suggested that Mary buy some nice clothes." (NOT "buys")

B Verbs & expressions that take the subjunctive

VERBS — demand / insist / propose / recommend / suggest
"The doctor recommended that I rest for a few days."
"John demanded that Hannah apologize to him."
IT'S ESSENTIAL / IMPORTANT / NECESSARY / VITAL (that) …
"It's essential that everyone be here on time."

Subjunctive — Negative & Common Mistakes Unit 32C–D

부정형 / suggest·insist 뒤 to 부정사 금지

📖 GIU Unit 32C–D, p.64-65

C Negative: not + base form

부정은 not + base form. 조동사 don't / doesn't 쓰지 않음.

"The doctor recommended that I not go to work for two days."
"It's very important that you not miss this appointment."

D Do NOT use to + infinitive after suggest / insist

✗ "Liz suggested Mary to buy…"   →   ✓ "Liz suggested that Mary buy…"
✗ "He insists to go with us."   →   ✓ "He insists on going with us."

I'd better ... Unit 33A–B

had better: 강한 조언/경고 (안 하면 문제가 생김)

📖 GIU Unit 33A–B, p.66-67

A had better = advisable; otherwise there will be a problem

Subject + 'd better (not) + base form
"I'd better go now or I'll be late."
"You'd better not go out tonight."

Remember: "had better" is past in form but present/future in meaning. Never use to — ✓ 'd better take (NOT 'd better to take).

B had better vs should

had better — specific + danger

"The movie starts at 8:30. You'd better go now, or you'll miss it."

should — general advice

"It's a great movie. You should go and see it." (no problem if you don't)

It's time ... Unit 33C

~할 때다 (지금 안 하면 늦다, 비판·재촉)

📖 GIU Unit 33C, p.66-67

C It's time ... / It's about time ...

IT'S TIME + TO / + 주어 + PAST FORM
"It's time to go home." = "It's time we went home."

과거형(went)이지만 현재 의미 — "이제 집에 갈 시간이다".

IT'S (ABOUT) TIME + 주어 + DID — 비판/재촉
"It's time you did something about it."
"It's about time he did something instead of just talking."

would Unit 34

would: 상상/가정 (현재·미래 & 과거)

📖 GIU Unit 34A, p.68-69

A would / wouldn't — imaginary situations (not real)

would ('d) / wouldn't + base form → imagine NOW/FUTURE
would have ('d have) / wouldn't have + past participle → imagine PAST
IMAGINE NOW / FUTURE — would / 'd
"It would be nice to buy a new car, but we can't afford it."   "I'd love to live by the ocean."
IMAGINE PAST — would have / 'd have
"They helped us a lot. I don't know what we'd have done without their help."
"It's a shame you didn't see the movie. You would have liked it."
COMPARE — would (now) vs would have (past)
"I would call Nicole, but I don't have her number." (now)   "I would have called Nicole, but I didn't have her number." (past)

would: will/would, refused, past habits Unit 34

will vs would 비교 | wouldn't = 거절 | 과거 습관

📖 GIU Unit 34B–C, p.68-69

B will ('ll) vs would ('d) & wouldn't = refused

WILL (real/can) vs WOULD (imagined/can't)
"I'll stay a little longer. I've got plenty of time."   "I'd stay a little longer, but I really have to go."
WOULD = REPORTED WILL (past of will)
Tom: "I'll call you on Sunday." → Tom said he'd call me on Sunday.
WOULDN'T = REFUSED (과거 거절)
"I tried to warn him, but he wouldn't listen to me."   "The car wouldn't start."

C would for past habits (= used to)

"When we were children, we would go to the beach every day in the summer." (= used to)
"Whenever Tony was angry, he would walk out of the room."

Can/Could you ...? & Can I have ...? Unit 35

요청 / 물건 요청

📖 GIU Unit 35A–B, p.70-71

A Asking people to do things (requests)

Can you ...? (informal)   →   Could you ...? (more polite)   →   Do you think you could ...? (tentative)
"Can you wait a minute, please?"   "Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the bus station?"
"Do you think you could take me to the airport?" (most tentative)

B Asking for things — Can I have ...? / Could I have ...? / May I have ...?

(in a store) "Can I have these postcards, please?" / "Can I get these postcards, please?"
(in a restaurant) "Could we have the menu, please?"   "May I have these postcards, please?" (most formal)

Permission, Offering & Inviting Unit 35

허가 요청, 제안과 초대

📖 GIU Unit 35C–D, p.70-71

C Asking to do something (permission)

(on the phone) "Hello, can I speak to Steve, please?"   "Could I use your phone charger?"
"May I ask you a question?" (more formal)
ALTERNATIVES — Do you mind if I ...? / Is it alright if I ...?
"Do you mind if I use your phone charger?"   "Is it alright if I sit here?" "Yes, of course."

D Offering and inviting

"Can I help you?" / "Can I get you some coffee?" (offer)
"Would you like some coffee?" / "Would you like to eat with us tonight?" (invitation)
"I'd like some information about hotels, please." (polite 'I want')

Obligation & Advice Scale

의무 & 조언 강도 척도

must / have to — Strong obligation 강한 의무
had better — Strong advice / warning 강한 조언 / 경고
should / ought to — Advice / recommendation 조언 / 권고
could / might — Suggestion (weaker) 제안 (더 약함)
don't have to — Not necessary 불필요 (해도 되고 안 해도 됨)
must not — Prohibited 금지

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

1/2

⚠ Mixing up must / have to

Korean "~해야 한다" doesn't distinguish between internal and external obligation. In English, the difference matters.

한국어 "~해야 한다"는 내적/외적 의무를 구분하지 않지만, 영어에서는 차이가 있습니다.

⚠ Overusing "should" where "would" is appropriate

"would"가 적절한 곳에 "should"를 과다 사용하는 실수.

"When I was a child, I should play outside every day."
"When I was a child, I would play outside every day."
Past habit = "would" or "used to," not "should." 과거 습관 = "would" 또는 "used to"

📖 GIU Units 30 & 34

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

2/2

⚠ Confusing mustn't and don't have to

"하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동.

"You mustn't bring food. It's optional."
"You don't have to bring food. It's optional."
"Optional" = not necessary = don't have to. "선택사항" = 필요 없음 = don't have to
