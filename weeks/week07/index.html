Must vs Have to

must vs have to: 의무의 차이

must (internal)

The speaker feels it is necessary. Personal feeling or decision.

화자가 필요하다고 느낌. 개인적 판단.

have to (external)

External rule, law, or requirement. Not the speaker's choice.

외부 규칙, 법, 요구사항. 화자의 선택이 아님.

Must vs Have to: Examples

must vs have to: 예문

must (internal)

"I must study harder."
I feel this way. 내가 그렇게 느낌.
"I must call my mother."
I personally feel I should. 개인적으로 해야 한다고 느낌.

have to (external)

"I have to wear a uniform."
It's a rule. 규칙임.
"You have to show your ID."
External requirement. 외부 요구사항.

Must not vs Don't have to

부정형: 금지 vs 불필요

The negatives are VERY different!

부정형의 의미가 매우 다릅니다!

must not (mustn't) = It is forbidden (prohibited)
don't have to = It is not necessary (optional)
"You mustn't use your phone during the exam."
It's prohibited! 금지! 하면 안 됨.
"You don't have to wear a suit. Casual is fine."
It's not necessary (but you can if you want). 필요 없음 (하지만 원하면 해도 됨).
⚠ Korean speakers often confuse these!

"~하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "~할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동하지 마세요!

Should

should: 조언, 의견, 권고

Subject + should + base form

Use should for advice, opinions, and recommendations. Weaker than "must."

조언, 의견, 권고에 사용합니다. "must"보다 약합니다.

"You should see a doctor."
Advice. 조언
"You shouldn't eat so much junk food."
Negative advice. 부정적 조언
"I think the government should invest more in education."
Opinion. 의견

Should have done

"~했어야 했는데" (과거에 대한 후회/비판)

Subject + should have + past participle

Something was the right thing to do, but you didn't do it.

올바른 행동이었지만 하지 않았음을 나타냅니다.

"I should have studied harder."
Regret: I didn't study enough. 후회: 충분히 공부하지 않았다.
"You shouldn't have said that."
Criticism: you said something wrong. 비판: 그런 말을 하지 말았어야 했다.

Subjunctive: suggest / recommend that...

접속법: suggest/recommend + that + 주어 + 동사원형

Special grammar after suggest, recommend, insist, demand, propose

suggest / recommend + that + subject + base form

The verb after "that" uses the base form (no -s, no past tense).

"that" 뒤의 동사는 원형을 사용합니다 (-s 없음, 과거형 아님).

"I suggest that he go to the hospital."
NOT "goes" — use the base form. "goes"가 아닌 원형 "go"를 사용.
"The teacher recommended that every student read the article."
NOT "reads" — subjunctive form. "reads"가 아닌 원형 "read"를 사용.
"I suggest that he goes to the hospital."
In formal English, this is incorrect. Use the base form. 격식체 영어에서는 원형을 사용해야 합니다.

Had better

had better: 강한 조언 / 경고

Subject + had better ('d better) + base form

Stronger than "should." Implies a warning — something bad will happen if you don't follow the advice.

"should"보다 강합니다. 경고의 의미 — 조언을 따르지 않으면 나쁜 일이 생깁니다.

"You'd better hurry or you'll miss the train."
Warning: bad consequence if you don't. 경고: 안 하면 나쁜 결과.
"We'd better not be late."
Negative: had better not + base form. 부정형: had better not + 동사원형.
Note: "had better" is for specific situations, not general advice. For general advice, use "should." "had better"는 특정 상황에 대한 것입니다. 일반적인 조언에는 "should"를 사용합니다.

Would

would: 과거 습관, 정중한 표현

1. Past habits (repeated actions)

Subject + would + base form

Similar to "used to" but ONLY for repeated actions (not states).

"used to"와 비슷하지만 반복적 행동에만 사용 (상태에는 사용 불가).

"When I was young, I would play in the park every day."
Repeated past action. 반복적인 과거 행동.
"I would be tall when I was young."
"Would" cannot be used for past states. Use "used to." 과거 상태에는 would를 사용할 수 없습니다.
"I used to be tall." (state → only "used to")

Would: Polite Expressions

would: 정중한 표현

2. Polite requests, offers, and preferences

"Would you like some coffee?"
Polite offer. 정중한 제안.
"I'd like to make a reservation."
Polite request. 정중한 요청.
"I'd rather stay home tonight."
Polite preference. 정중한 선호.

Obligation & Advice Scale

의무 & 조언 강도 척도

must / have to — Strong obligation 강한 의무
had better — Strong advice / warning 강한 조언 / 경고
should / ought to — Advice / recommendation 조언 / 권고
could / might — Suggestion (weaker) 제안 (더 약함)
don't have to — Not necessary 불필요 (해도 되고 안 해도 됨)
must not — Prohibited 금지

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ Mixing up must / have to

Korean "~해야 한다" doesn't distinguish between internal and external obligation. In English, the difference matters.

한국어 "~해야 한다"는 내적/외적 의무를 구분하지 않지만, 영어에서는 차이가 있습니다.

⚠ Overusing "should" where "would" is appropriate

"would"가 적절한 곳에 "should"를 과다 사용하는 실수.

"When I was a child, I should play outside every day."
"When I was a child, I would play outside every day."
Past habit = "would" or "used to," not "should." 과거 습관 = "would" 또는 "used to"
⚠ Confusing mustn't and don't have to

"하면 안 된다" (mustn't)와 "할 필요 없다" (don't have to)를 혼동.

"You mustn't bring food. It's optional."
"You don't have to bring food. It's optional."
"Optional" = not necessary = don't have to. "선택사항" = 필요 없음 = don't have to
