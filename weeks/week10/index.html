Passive Voice — Basic Formula

수동태 기본 공식

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 40 (p.80–81)

How to Form the Passive

The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.

Subject + be (correct tense) + past participle (+ by agent)
Active: "Somebody cleans this room every day."
Passive: "This room is cleaned every day."
The agent ("somebody") is removed because it's not important. 행위자("somebody")가 중요하지 않으므로 제거됩니다.
"This house was built in 1981."
Compare: "My grandfather was a builder. He built this house in 1981." (active) 비교: "할아버지는 건축가였습니다. 이 집을 1981년에 지었습니다." (능동)

Passive Across Tenses

시제별 수동태

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Units 40–41 (p.80–83)

Present Simple
"This room is cleaned every day."
Past Simple
"This house was built in 1981."
Present Perfect
"My car has been repaired. You can use it now."
Past Perfect
"The vegetables didn't taste good. They had been cooked too long."
Going to
"The house is going to be knocked down."
Modal
"The situation is serious. Something must be done."

Continuous Passive Forms

진행형 수동태

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 41 (p.82–83)

be + being + past participle
Present Continuous Passive
"The office is being cleaned right now."
Active: "Someone is cleaning the office right now." 능동: "누군가가 지금 사무실을 청소하고 있다."
Present Perfect Passive
"Have you ever been bitten by a dog?"
Present perfect passive: have/has + been + past participle 현재완료 수동태: have/has + been + 과거분사
Modal Passive
"The meeting has been canceled."
Also: "The situation is serious. Something must be done." "상황이 심각합니다. 무언가 조치가 취해져야 합니다."

Passive 3 — born / get / being done

수동태 3 — born / get / being done

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 42 (p.84–85)

"be born"
"I was born in Chicago." / "Where were you born?"
Always past tense: "was/were born" (NOT "am born"). 항상 과거형: "was/were born" ("am born"이 아님).
"get + past participle"
"There was a fight, but nobody got hurt."
"I don't get invited to many parties."
"get" replaces "be" in informal English. "get"이 비격식체에서 "be" 대신 사용됩니다.
"being + past participle"
"I don't like being told what to do."
"I remember being taken to the zoo when I was a child."
After verbs like "like," "remember," "hate" + passive gerund. "like," "remember," "hate" 뒤에 수동 동명사가 옵니다.
Two-object verbs
"I was given this watch." = "This watch was given to me."
"I've been offered the job, but I don't think I want it." 두 개의 목적어가 있는 동사는 두 가지 수동태가 가능합니다.

Reporting Structures

보고 구문 — It is said that... / He is said to...

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 43 (p.86–87)

Two Passive Reporting Patterns

It + is said/believed/known/reported + that + clause
Subject + is said/believed + to + base form (or to have + pp)
"It is said that he is 108 years old."
"He is said to be 108 years old."
Both sentences mean the same thing. The second is more formal. 두 문장의 의미는 같습니다. 두 번째가 더 격식체.
"You're not supposed to park your car here."
"supposed to" = expected or required to do something. "supposed to" = ~하기로 되어 있다

Have / Get Something Done

사역 구문 — 누군가에게 무언가를 시키다

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 44 (p.88–89)

Causative Passive

Use this when you arrange for someone else to do something for you.

Subject + have/get + object + past participle
"Megan had the roof repaired." (she arranged for someone to do it)
Compare: "Megan repaired the roof." = she did it herself. 비교: "Megan repaired the roof." = 직접 수리.
"Your hair looks nice. Did you have it cut?"
"get something done" is more informal than "have something done." "get something done"은 더 비격식적입니다.
"Nick had his nose broken in a fight."
Different meaning! Something bad happened to him (not arranged). 다른 뜻! 나쁜 일이 일어났다는 뜻 (의뢰 아님).

Active vs. Passive

능동태 vs. 수동태 비교

Grammar in Use Intermediate 4th Ed. — Unit 40 (p.80–81)

Active Voice

"My grandfather built this house in 1981."

"Somebody cleaned this room yesterday."

Focus on WHO does the action

Passive Voice

"This house was built in 1981."

"This room was cleaned yesterday."

Focus on WHAT happened (agent often omitted)

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean uses passive markers (-되다/-지다) differently. Students often overuse "by" or forget "be".

한국어의 수동태 '-되다/-지다'와 영어 수동태의 사용 맥락이 다릅니다.

"The cake was eaten by me." → "The cake was eaten."
Only use "by..." when the agent is important. 행위자가 중요할 때만 "by..."를 사용.
Missing "be" in Passive
"The window broken yesterday." → "The window was broken yesterday."
Passive always needs a form of "be." 수동태에는 반드시 "be"가 필요합니다.

When to Use the Passive

수동태를 사용하는 경우

1. Agent is unknown

"My bike was stolen."

행위자를 모를 때

2. Agent is obvious

"He was arrested."

행위자가 명백할 때 (경찰)

3. Agent is unimportant

"The building was completed in 2020."

행위자가 중요하지 않을 때

4. Formal / academic writing

"The data were analyzed using SPSS."
