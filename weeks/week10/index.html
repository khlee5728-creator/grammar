Passive Voice — Basic Formula

수동태 기본 공식

How to Form the Passive

The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.

Subject + be (correct tense) + past participle (+ by agent)
Active: "Someone cleans the room every day."
Passive: "The room is cleaned every day."
The agent ("someone") is removed because it's not important. 행위자("someone")가 중요하지 않으므로 제거됩니다.

Passive Across Tenses

시제별 수동태

Present Simple
"The room is cleaned every day."
Past Simple
"The bridge was built in 1990."
Present Perfect
"My car has been stolen!"
Past Perfect
"The food had been prepared before we arrived."
Future (will)
"The project will be finished tomorrow."
Modal
"This problem can be solved."

Continuous Passive Forms

진행형 수동태

be + being + past participle
Present Continuous Passive
"The house is being painted right now."
Active: "They are painting the house right now." 능동: "그들이 지금 집을 칠하고 있다."
Past Continuous Passive
"The road was being repaired when I drove by."
Active: "They were repairing the road when I drove by." 능동: "내가 지나갈 때 그들이 도로를 수리하고 있었다."

Reporting Structures

보고 구문 — It is said that... / He is said to...

Two Passive Reporting Patterns

It + is said/believed/known/reported + that + clause
Subject + is said/believed + to + base form (or to have + pp)
"It is believed that he is very wealthy."
"He is believed to be very wealthy."
Both sentences mean the same thing. The second pattern is more formal. 두 문장의 의미는 같습니다. 두 번째 패턴이 더 격식체입니다.
"She is reported to have left the country."
"to have left" = referring to a past event. "to have left" = 과거 사건을 언급

Have / Get Something Done

사역 구문 — 누군가에게 무언가를 시키다

Causative Passive

Use this when you arrange for someone else to do something for you.

Subject + have/get + object + past participle
"I had my hair cut."
Someone cut my hair for me (I didn't cut it myself). 누군가가 나를 위해 머리를 잘라줬습니다 (내가 직접 자른 것이 아닙니다).
"I got my car repaired."
"get something done" is more informal than "have something done." "get something done"은 "have something done"보다 비격식적입니다.
"I cut my hair."
This means YOU cut your own hair! 이것은 자기가 직접 머리를 잘랐다는 뜻입니다!

Active vs. Passive

능동태 vs. 수동태 비교

Active Voice

"Shakespeare wrote Hamlet."

"They are building a new bridge."

"Someone has stolen my wallet."

Focus on WHO does the action

Passive Voice

"Hamlet was written by Shakespeare."

"A new bridge is being built."

"My wallet has been stolen."

Focus on WHAT happened (agent often omitted)

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean uses passive markers (-되다/-지다) differently from English. Students often overuse "by" or avoid passive entirely.

한국어의 수동태 '-되다/-지다'와 영어 수동태의 사용 맥락이 다릅니다.

"The cake was eaten by me."
Unnecessary "by" phrase — the agent is obvious or unimportant.
"The cake was eaten."
Only use "by..." when the agent is important or surprising. 행위자가 중요하거나 놀라운 경우에만 "by..."를 사용합니다.
Missing "be" in Passive
"The window broken yesterday."
"The window was broken yesterday."
Don't forget "be" — passive always needs a form of "be." 수동태에는 반드시 "be"가 필요합니다.

When to Use the Passive

수동태를 사용하는 경우

1. Agent is unknown

"My bike was stolen."

행위자를 모를 때

2. Agent is obvious

"He was arrested."

행위자가 명백할 때 (경찰)

3. Agent is unimportant

"The building was completed in 2020."

행위자가 중요하지 않을 때

4. Formal / academic writing

"The data were analyzed using SPSS."

격식체/학술적 글쓰기
