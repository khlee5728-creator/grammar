Present Perfect: Formation

현재완료: 형태

How to form the present perfect

Subject + have / has + past participle (V3)

The past participle of regular verbs ends in -ed. Irregular verbs have unique forms (gone, seen, written, etc.).

규칙 동사의 과거분사는 -ed로 끝납니다. 불규칙 동사는 고유한 형태가 있습니다.

Present Perfect: Forms

Positive

I have worked / She has worked

Negative

I have not (haven't) worked / She has not (hasn't) worked

Question

Have you worked...? / Has she worked...?

Present Perfect on the Timeline

타임라인으로 보는 현재완료

The present perfect connects a past action or state to the present moment.

"I have lived here since 2015."
Started in 2015 and continues now. 2015년에 시작해서 지금까지 계속됨

Use 1: Experience

용법 1: 경험

We use the present perfect to talk about experiences in our life (time not specified).

삶에서의 경험을 이야기할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다 (시간이 명시되지 않음).

"Have you ever + past participle...?"
"I have been to Japan."
At some point in my life, I visited Japan. 내 인생에서 어느 시점에 일본을 방문했다.
"Have you ever eaten sushi?"
At any time in your life? 살면서 한 번이라도?

Use 2: Recent Completion

용법 2: 최근 완료

We use the present perfect (often with just, already, yet) for recently completed actions.

최근에 완료된 행동에 현재완료를 사용합니다 (주로 just, already, yet과 함께).

"I've just finished my homework."
Completed a short time ago. 조금 전에 완료됨
"She has already left."
Before now / before expected. 지금 이전에 / 예상보다 일찍
"Have you finished yet?"
Used in questions and negatives. 의문문과 부정문에 사용

Use 3: Change Over Time

용법 3: 시간 경과에 따른 변화

We use the present perfect to describe changes that have happened over a period of time.

일정 기간 동안 일어난 변화를 설명할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다.

"Your English has improved."
Compared to before, it is better now. 이전에 비해 지금 나아졌다.
"The population of Seoul has grown."
Change from past to present. 과거에서 현재까지의 변화

Present Perfect Continuous

현재완료 진행형

Form

Subject + have/has been + verb-ing

Use this to emphasize the duration or ongoing nature of an activity that started in the past and continues to the present.

과거에 시작되어 현재까지 계속되는 활동의 지속이나 진행 중인 성격을 강조할 때 사용합니다.

"I have been studying English for three years."
The activity is ongoing. 활동이 계속 진행 중이다.
"It has been raining since morning."
Started in the morning and is still raining now. 아침에 시작해서 지금도 비가 오고 있다.

Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous

현재완료 단순형 vs 진행형

have done (Simple)

Focuses on the result or completion.

"I've read the book."
Finished reading it. 다 읽었다 (결과 강조)
"She's written five emails."
Quantity completed. 완료된 수량

have been doing (Continuous)

Focuses on the activity / duration.

"I've been reading the book."
Still reading / activity in progress. 아직 읽는 중 (활동 강조)
"She's been writing emails all day."
Duration / ongoing activity. 지속 / 진행 중인 활동

For vs Since

for vs since

for + duration

기간 (얼마나 오래)

for two years
for a long time
for 30 minutes
for ages

since + starting point

시작 시점 (언제부터)

since 2020
since Monday
since I was a child
since breakfast
"We have known each other for ten years / since 2015."

How long have you...?

"얼마나 오래 ~했어?" 질문하기

"How long have you known her?"

Use present perfect (simple or continuous) to ask about duration up to now.

지금까지의 기간에 대해 질문할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다.

"How long have you been waiting?"
"Since 3 o'clock." / "For about 20 minutes."
"How long have you lived in Seoul?"
"For five years." / "Since 2020."

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ L1 Interference: Using Simple Past instead of Present Perfect

Korean uses past tense where English requires present perfect when the result is relevant NOW.

한국어에서는 과거형으로 표현하지만, 결과가 현재에 영향을 미치면 현재완료를 씁니다.

"I lost my key. I can't find it."
Korean: "나 열쇠 잃어버렸어" → past tense feels natural
"I've lost my key. I can't find it."
The key is still lost NOW → present perfect needed. 열쇠를 아직 못 찾은 상태 → 현재완료 필요
"Did you ever go to Europe?"
"Have you ever been to Europe?"
Life experience → present perfect. 인생 경험 → 현재완료

More Common Mistakes

추가 주의사항

⚠ Confusing for / since

for와 since를 혼동하는 실수

"I have lived here since three years."
"I have lived here for three years."
"Three years" is a duration → use for. "3년"은 기간 → for 사용
⚠ Using present perfect with specific past time

특정 과거 시점과 현재완료를 함께 쓰는 실수

"I have seen that movie last week."
"I saw that movie last week."
Specific past time (last week) → use simple past. 특정 과거 시점 → 단순과거 사용

Week 3 Summary

3주차 요약

  • Form: have/has + past participle (V3) 형태: have/has + 과거분사
  • Experience: "Have you ever...?" (time not important) 경험: 시간이 중요하지 않음
  • Recent completion: just, already, yet 최근 완료: just, already, yet
  • Change over time: "It has improved." 시간 경과에 따른 변화
  • Continuous: have been + verb-ing (focus on duration/activity) 진행형: 지속/활동에 초점
  • for = duration / since = starting point for = 기간 / since = 시작 시점
