Units 7 – 12

Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Understand the form and meaning of the present perfect tense 현재완료 시제의 형태와 의미를 이해한다
  • Use present perfect for experience, recent completion, and change over time 경험, 최근 완료, 시간 경과에 따른 변화를 현재완료로 표현한다
  • Distinguish between present perfect simple and continuous 현재완료 단순형과 진행형을 구분한다

Present Perfect: Formation

현재완료: 형태

How to form the present perfect

Subject + have / has + past participle (V3)

The past participle of regular verbs ends in -ed. Irregular verbs have unique forms (gone, seen, written, etc.).

규칙 동사의 과거분사는 -ed로 끝납니다. 불규칙 동사는 고유한 형태가 있습니다.

Present Perfect: Forms

Positive

I have worked / She has worked

Negative

I have not (haven’t) worked / She has not (hasn’t) worked

Question

Have you worked…? / Has she worked…?

📘 GIU Unit 7, p.14-15

Present Perfect on the Timeline

타임라인으로 보는 현재완료

The present perfect connects a past action or state to the present moment.

“I have lived here since 2015.”
Started in 2015 and continues now. 2015년에 시작해서 지금까지 계속됨

Use 1: Experience

용법 1: 경험

We use the present perfect to talk about experiences in our life (time not specified).

삶에서의 경험을 이야기할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다 (시간이 명시되지 않음).

“Have you ever + past participle…?”
“Have you traveled a lot, Liz?” “Yes, I’ve been to lots of places.”
Life experience — no specific time mentioned. 인생 경험 — 특정 시간이 언급되지 않음.
“Have you ever been to China?” “Yes, I’ve been to China twice.”
Asking about experience at any time in life. 살면서 한 번이라도 경험했는지 묻기.
“I’ve met a lot of people in the last few days.”
Period up to now (the last few days). 현재까지의 기간 (최근 며칠 동안).

📘 GIU Unit 7, p.14-15

Use 2: Recent Completion

용법 2: 최근 완료

We use the present perfect (often with just, already, yet) for recently completed actions with present relevance.

최근에 완료된 행동에 현재완료를 사용합니다 (주로 just, already, yet과 함께).

just: “Are you hungry?” “No, I’ve just had lunch.”
Completed a very short time ago. 아주 조금 전에 완료됨
already: “He’s already left.”
Before now / before expected. 지금 이전에 / 예상보다 일찍
yet: “Has it stopped raining yet?” / “She hasn’t left yet.”
Used in questions and negatives — “up to now.” 의문문과 부정문에 사용

📘 GIU Unit 8, p.16-17

Present Perfect vs Simple Past

현재완료 vs 단순과거

Use present perfect when the result matters NOW. Use simple past for completed actions in a finished time period.

결과가 현재에 중요하면 현재완료, 완료된 과거 시간이면 단순과거를 사용합니다.

“Sally has had a baby! It’s a boy.”
News — present relevance. 새로운 소식 — 현재에 영향
“Mozart was a composer. He wrote more than 600 pieces of music.”
Finished time period (Mozart is dead) → simple past. 완료된 시간 → 단순과거
“I’ve had four cups of coffee today.”
“Today” is not finished yet → present perfect. “오늘”은 아직 안 끝남 → 현재완료

📘 GIU Unit 8, p.16-17

Use 3: Change Over Time

용법 3: 시간 경과에 따른 변화

We use the present perfect to describe changes that have happened over a period of time.

일정 기간 동안 일어난 변화를 설명할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다.

“Your English has improved.”
Compared to before, it is better now. 이전에 비해 지금 나아졌다.
“The population of Seoul has grown.”
Change from past to present. 과거에서 현재까지의 변화
“Have you heard anything from Ben recently?”
Period up to now (recently). 현재까지의 기간 (최근에)

📘 GIU Unit 7, p.14-15

Present Perfect Continuous

현재완료 진행형

Form

Subject + have/has been + verb-ing

Use this to emphasize the duration or ongoing nature of an activity that started in the past and continues to the present.

과거에 시작되어 현재까지 계속되는 활동의 지속이나 진행 중인 성격을 강조할 때 사용합니다.

“Why are you out of breath? Have you been running?”
Evidence of recent activity. 최근 활동의 증거.
“How long has it been raining?” “It’s been raining for two hours.”
Asking about duration of an ongoing activity. 진행 중인 활동의 지속 시간 물어보기.
“How long have you been learning English?”
Activity started in the past and continues now. 과거에 시작해서 지금도 계속되는 활동.

📘 GIU Unit 9, p.18-19

Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous

현재완료 단순형 vs 진행형

have done (Simple)

Focuses on the result or completion.

“My bike is OK again now. I’ve repaired it.”
Result: the bike is fixed. 결과: 자전거가 고쳐짐

have been doing (Continuous)

Focuses on the activity / duration.

“My hands are very dirty. I’ve been repairing my bike.”
Evidence of the activity itself. 활동 자체의 증거

📘 GIU Unit 10, p.20-21

How long have you…?

“얼마나 오래 ~했어?” 질문하기

“How long have you known her?”

Use present perfect (simple or continuous) to ask about duration up to now.

지금까지의 기간에 대해 질문할 때 현재완료를 사용합니다.

“They have been married for 20 years.”
State that started in the past and continues now. 과거에 시작되어 지금까지 계속되는 상태.
“He’s been in the hospital since Monday.”
Still in the hospital now. 지금도 병원에 있다.
“I’ve been learning English since January.”
Started in January, still learning now. 1월에 시작해서 지금도 배우는 중.

📘 GIU Unit 11, p.22-23

For vs Since

for vs since

for + duration

기간 (얼마나 오래)

for two hours
for six months
for a long time

since + starting point

시작 시점 (언제부터)

since 8:00
since April
since I was a child
“We’ve been waiting for two hours.” / “We’ve been waiting since 8:00.”

📘 GIU Unit 12, p.24-25

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ L1 Interference: Using Simple Past instead of Present Perfect

Korean uses past tense where English requires present perfect when the result is relevant NOW.

한국어에서는 과거형으로 표현하지만, 결과가 현재에 영향을 미치면 현재완료를 씁니다.

“I lost my key. I can’t find it.”
Korean: “나 열쇠 잃어버렸어” → past tense feels natural
“I’ve lost my key. I can’t find it.”
The key is still lost NOW → present perfect needed. 열쇠를 아직 못 찾은 상태 → 현재완료 필요
“Did you ever go to Europe?”
“Have you ever been to Europe?”
Life experience → present perfect. 인생 경험 → 현재완료

📘 GIU Unit 8, p.16-17

More Common Mistakes

추가 주의사항

⚠ Confusing for / since

for와 since를 혼동하는 실수

“I have lived here since three years.”
“I have lived here for three years.”
“Three years” is a duration → use for. “3년”은 기간 → for 사용
⚠ Using present perfect with specific past time

특정 과거 시점과 현재완료를 함께 쓰는 실수

“My mother has grown up in Italy.” → “My mother grew up in Italy.”
Finished period of life → simple past. 완료된 인생 시기 → 단순과거

📘 GIU Unit 12, p.24-25

Present Perfect Continuous: Compare

현재완료 진행형: 비교하기

Present Continuous

“Don’t disturb me now. I’m working.”
Happening right now. 지금 진행 중.

Present Perfect Continuous

“I’ve been working hard. Now I’m going to have a break.”
Activity over a period, now finished or pausing. 일정 기간 동안의 활동, 지금 끝남/잠시 멈춤.

Week 3 Summary

3주차 요약

  • Form: have/has + past participle (V3) 형태: have/has + 과거분사
  • Experience: “Have you ever…?” (time not important) 경험: 시간이 중요하지 않음
  • Recent completion: just, already, yet 최근 완료: just, already, yet
  • Change over time: “It has improved.” 시간 경과에 따른 변화
  • Continuous: have been + verb-ing (focus on duration/activity) 진행형: 지속/활동에 초점
  • for = duration / since = starting point for = 기간 / since = 시작 시점
