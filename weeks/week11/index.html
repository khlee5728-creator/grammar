Reported Speech 1 — Examples Unit 45

간접 화법 예문

Present → Past
Dan said, "I'm feeling sick." → Dan said he was feeling sick.
Will → Would / Can → Could
"I'll call you." → She said she would call me.
"I can't come." → She said she couldn't come.
Past simple → Past simple OR Past perfect
"I woke up sick." → He said he woke up sick. OR he had woken up sick.
Past simple can stay the same or change to past perfect.

📖 GIU Unit 45A-C, p.90

Reported Speech 2 — say and tell Unit 46C-D

say와 tell의 구분 / 간접 명령문

say vs. tell

With a person → tell: Emma told me that you were sick.

Without a person → say: Emma said that you were sick.

tell / ask somebody to...

tell + somebody + (not) to...
"Drink water." → The doctor told me to drink water.
"Don't work too hard." → I told Joe not to work too hard.
"Can you help me?" → Jackie asked me to help her.

📖 GIU Unit 46, p.92–93

Questions 2 (Do you know where ...?) Unit 48

간접 의문문

Key Rule: Statement word order (no inversion, no "do/did")

asked + question word + subject + verb
Embedded: Do you know + question word + subject + verb
"Where are you going?" → He asked us where we were going.
"What time does it start?" → Do you know what time it starts?
Drop do/does/did in embedded questions. 내포 의문문: do/does/did 제거.

📖 GIU Unit 48, p.96

Questions 1 — Who/What as Subject Unit 47B

who/what이 주어인 의문문

Do NOT use do/does/did when who/what/which is the subject

📖 "But do not use do/does/did if who/what, etc. is the subject of the sentence." (Unit 47B)

Who + verb …?   (NOT “Who does/did + verb?”)
who = object → use did
Emma called somebody. → Who did Emma call?
who = subject → NO did
Somebody called Emma. → Who called Emma?
"who"가 주어이면 do/does/did를 쓰지 않습니다.
Who wants something to eat? (NOT “Who does want”)
What happened to you last night? (NOT “What did happen”)

📖 GIU Unit 47, p.94

Tag Questions (do you? isn't it?, etc.) Unit 50

부가 의문문

Positive → negative tag / Negative → positive tag

Statement + , + auxiliary + (not) + pronoun?
"Kate will be here soon, won't she?" 긍정 → 부정
"You're tired, aren't you?"
"They don't like us, do they?" 부정 → 긍정
"You haven't eaten yet, have you?"

📖 GIU Unit 50, p.100

Tag Questions — Special Cases Unit 50D

부가 의문문 — 특수한 경우

"I'm..., aren't I?"
"I'm right, aren't I?"
After "I'm...", the negative tag is "aren't I?" (not "am I not?"). "I'm..." 뒤에는 "aren't I?"를 사용합니다.
Imperative → will you?
"Listen to me, will you?"
After Do / Listen / Give, etc. (imperative), the tag is usually "will you?" 명령문(Do/Listen/Give 등) 뒤에는 보통 "will you?"를 사용합니다.
"Let's..., shall we?"
"Let's go, shall we?"
Nobody/nothing/no one → positive tag
"Nobody called, did they?"
Negative words like "nobody" make the sentence negative, so the tag is positive. "nobody"와 같은 부정어는 문장을 부정문으로 만들므로 태그는 긍정입니다.

📖 GIU Unit 50, p.100

Auxiliary Verbs (have/do/can, etc.) / I think so Unit 49

조동사: So/Neither 패턴 & I think so 패턴

So + auxiliary + subject (= me too) / Neither + auxiliary + subject (= me neither)

📖 "Note the word order after so and neither (verb before subject)." (Unit 49C)

"I'm tired." — "So am I."  |  "I never read newspapers." — "Neither do I."
So...
"I passed the exam." — "So did Paul." (= Paul도 합격했다)
Neither...
"I can't cook." — "Neither can Tom." (= Tom도 못 한다)
Note: verb comes before subject after so/neither. so/neither 뒤에는 동사가 주어 앞에 옵니다 (도치).

I think so / I hope not

"Are those people Korean?" — "I think so." (= I think they are Korean)

Negatives: I don't think so / I hope not / I'm afraid not

📖 GIU Unit 49, p.98

Direct vs. Reported Speech Units 45–46

직접 화법 vs. 간접 화법

Direct Speech

Dan said, "I'm feeling sick."

Anna said, "I'll call you when I get back."

Uses quotation marks, exact words, original tenses

Reported Speech

Dan said he was feeling sick.

Anna said she would call me when she got back.

No quotation marks, tense backshift, pronoun changes

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference: No Tense Backshift
"He said he is tired." → "He said he was tired."
Backshift: "is" → "was" 시제 후퇴: "is" → "was"
L1 Interference: Question Word Order
"He asked where did I live." → "He asked where I lived."
No inversion, no "did" — statement word order. 도치/did 없이 평서문 어순.

Week 11 Summary

11주차 요약

  • Tense backshift: present→past, past→past perfect, will→would, can→could
  • Say vs. tell: tell + somebody; say (no person); tell/ask somebody to...
  • Reported questions: statement word order, no "do/did"; embedded: Do you know where...?
  • Who/what subject: Who called Emma? (no did) vs. Who did Emma call?
  • Question tags: positive→negative tag, negative→positive tag; I'm..., aren't I?
  • So/Neither: "So am I" / "Neither do I"; I think so / I hope not
