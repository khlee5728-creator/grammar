Reported Speech Examples

간접 화법 예문

Present → Past
Direct: She said, "I am tired."
Reported: She said (that) she was tired.
현재형 "am" → 과거형 "was"
Will → Would
Direct: He said, "I'll come tomorrow."
Reported: He said he would come the next day.
Note: "tomorrow" also changes to "the next day." "tomorrow"도 "the next day"로 바뀝니다.
Past → Past Perfect
Direct: "I saw the movie."
Reported: She said she had seen the movie.

Reported Questions

간접 의문문

Key Rule: Use statement word order (no inversion, no "do/did")

asked + question word + subject + verb (statement order)
Yes/No questions: asked + if / whether + subject + verb
Direct: "Where do you live?"
Reported: He asked where I lived.
No "did" — use statement word order. "did"를 쓰지 않고, 평서문 어순을 사용합니다.
Direct: "Do you like coffee?"
Reported: She asked if I liked coffee.
For yes/no questions, add "if" or "whether." 예/아니오 의문문에는 "if" 또는 "whether"를 추가합니다.

Question Tags

부가 의문문

Positive statement → negative tag / Negative statement → positive tag

Statement + , + auxiliary + (not) + pronoun?
"You're a student, aren't you?"
Positive statement → negative tag 긍정문 → 부정 태그
"She can swim, can't she?"
"You don't like it, do you?"
Negative statement → positive tag 부정문 → 긍정 태그
"They haven't finished, have they?"

Question Tags — Special Cases

부가 의문문 — 특수한 경우

"I am..., aren't I?"
"I'm late, aren't I?"
Special: "am I not?" is very formal; "aren't I?" is standard. "am I not?"은 매우 격식적; "aren't I?"가 일반적입니다.
Imperative sentences
"Open the door, will you?"
Imperative tags use "will you?" or "won't you?" 명령문 태그는 "will you?" 또는 "won't you?"를 사용합니다.
"Let's..., shall we?"
"Let's go, shall we?"
Nobody/nothing/no one → positive tag
"Nobody called, did they?"
Negative words like "nobody" make the sentence negative, so the tag is positive. "nobody"와 같은 부정어는 문장을 부정문으로 만들므로 태그는 긍정입니다.

Direct vs. Reported Speech

직접 화법 vs. 간접 화법

Direct Speech

"I am happy," she said.

"I will help you," he said.

"Where do you work?" she asked.

Uses quotation marks, exact words

Reported Speech

She said she was happy.

He said he would help me.

She asked where I worked.

No quotation marks, tense backshift, word order changes

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference: No Tense Backshift

Korean does not change tenses when reporting speech. Korean speakers often keep the original tense.

한국어는 간접 화법에서 시제를 변화시키지 않습니다.

"He said he is tired."
"He said he was tired."
Backshift: present "is" → past "was" 시제 후퇴: 현재 "is" → 과거 "was"
L1 Interference: Question Word Order

Korean speakers often keep question word order in reported questions.

"He asked where did I live."
"He asked where I lived."
No inversion, no "did" — use statement word order. 도치 없이, "did" 없이 — 평서문 어순을 사용합니다.
