Verb + -ing (enjoy doing / stop doing, etc.) Unit 51

동명사(-ing)가 뒤따르는 동사

These verbs are ALWAYS followed by -ing

📖 "After enjoy, mind, and suggest, we use -ing (not to ...)." (Unit 51A)

enjoy, mind, suggest, avoid, finish, practice, consider, imagine, deny, admit, risk, keep, give up
"I enjoy reading." (not "enjoy to read")
"Would you mind closing the door?"
"Chris suggested going to the movies."
"He tried to avoid answering my question."

📖 "After these verbs you can use to ... (infinitive): offer, plan, manage, deserve, afford, learn, tend..." (Unit 52A)

Verb (+ Object) + to ... (I want you to ...) Unit 53

동사 + 목적어 + to부정사

verb + object + to + infinitive

tell, advise, remind, warn, invite, encourage, persuade, get, force, allow + somebody + to...
"We expected Dan to be late."
"I wouldn't advise you to stay there."
"Can you remind me to call Sam tomorrow?"
"They don't allow people to park here."

make / let + somebody + bare infinitive (no "to"!)

"Hot weather makes me feel tired."  |  "Her parents wouldn't let her go out alone."

Verb + -ing or to ... 1 (remember, regret, etc.) Unit 54

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (1)

remember + -ing = memory of the past

remember + to = don't forget to do (future)

📖 "Some verbs can be followed by -ing or to ... with a difference of meaning." (Unit 54B)

remember + -ing (past memory)
"I know I locked the door. I clearly remember locking it."
I did it, and now I remember this.
remember + to (future task)
"I remembered to lock the door, but I forgot to shut the windows."
I remembered that I had to do it, so I did it.
"Remember to buy some bananas." = Don't forget to buy them.

📖 GIU Unit 54, p.108-109

Verb + -ing or to ... 2 (try, need, help) Unit 55

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (2)

try + -ing = experiment

"Try pressing the green button."
Do it as an experiment or test. 실험 삼아 해보세요. (실험)

try + to = attempt (difficult)

"I was very tired. I tried to keep my eyes open, but I couldn't."
I attempted to do it, but it was difficult. 시도했지만 어려웠습니다. (시도)

stop + -ing = quit

"He stopped smoking."
He gave up the habit. 그 습관을 그만뒀습니다. (중단)

stop + to = pause in order to

"He stopped to smoke."
He paused what he was doing in order to smoke. 하던 일을 멈추고 담배를 피우기 위해. (목적)

📖 GIU Unit 55, p.110-111

Verb + -ing or to ... 1 — regret Unit 54B

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (3)

regret + -ing = past regret

"I wish I hadn't done it."

regret + to = formal present

"I'm sorry to say..."

regret + -ing (past regret)
"I regret telling her the secret."
I told her, and now I wish I hadn't.
regret + to (formal announcement)
"I regret to inform you that your application was unsuccessful."
Formal: "I'm sorry to tell you..." 격식적 표현

📖 GIU Unit 54, p.108-109

Verb + -ing or to ... 3 (like / would like, etc.) Unit 56

like / would like 뒤에 오는 -ing와 to부정사

Repeated actions: -ing or to... (no difference)

"Do you like getting up early?" = "Do you like to get up early?"

Existing situation: -ing only

"Do you like being a student?" (not "to be")

would like / would love + to...

"I'd like to go away for a few days."
"I wouldn't like to go on vacation alone."

would mind + -ing

"Would you mind closing the door?"
"Would you mind waiting a few minutes?"

📖 GIU Unit 56, p.112-113

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

1/2

L1 Interference Warning

Korean does not distinguish between -ing and to after verbs. Memorize which verbs take which form.

"I enjoy to play tennis."
"I enjoy playing tennis." — enjoy is ALWAYS + -ing
"She avoided to speak in public."
"She avoided speaking in public." — avoid is ALWAYS + -ing

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

2/2

Using -ing with "to" verbs
"I decided going to the party."
"I decided to go to the party." — decide + to + infinitive
Confusing meaning-changing verbs
"Remember buying milk." (intended: don't forget)
"Remember to buy milk."
remember + to = don't forget; remember + -ing = recall a past event

Week 12 Summary

  • Verb + -ing: enjoy, mind, suggest, avoid, finish, consider, deny, risk, give up, keep
  • Verb + to: decide, agree, fail, tend, afford, manage, refuse, promise, learn, hope
  • Verb + object + to: tell/advise/warn/allow sb to... | make/let + bare infinitive
  • remember/forget + -ing = past memory; + to = future task
  • try + -ing = experiment; try + to = attempt | need -ing = need to be done
  • like/love/hate: -ing or to (repeated); -ing only (existing) | would like + to
  • Korean speakers: memorize which verbs take -ing vs. to
