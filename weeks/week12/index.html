Verbs Followed by -ing

동명사(-ing)가 뒤따르는 동사

These verbs are ALWAYS followed by -ing

enjoy, mind, suggest, avoid, finish, practice, consider, imagine, deny, admit, risk, keep, give up
"I enjoy reading."
(not "I enjoy to read") ("I enjoy to read"는 틀립니다)
"Would you mind closing the door?"
"Chris suggested going to the movies."
"He tried to avoid answering my question."

Verbs Followed by to...

to부정사가 뒤따르는 동사

These verbs are ALWAYS followed by to + infinitive

want, need, decide, hope, expect, plan, offer, agree, refuse, learn, promise, seem, pretend, afford, manage
"We decided to take a taxi home."
"I agreed to help him."
"I waved to Emily, but failed to attract her attention."

"We decided not to go out because of the weather."
The negative is not to... 부정형은 not to...입니다

Verb + Object + to...

동사 + 목적어 + to부정사 (I want you to...)

verb + object + to + infinitive

tell, advise, remind, warn, invite, encourage, persuade, get, force, teach, allow, enable + somebody + to...
"We expected Dan to be late."
"I wouldn't advise you to stay there."
"Can you remind me to call Sam tomorrow?"
"They don't allow people to park in front of the building."

make / let + somebody + bare infinitive (no "to"!)

"Hot weather makes me feel tired."  |  "Her parents wouldn't let her go out alone."
make/let 뒤에는 to 없이 동사 원형이 옵니다

📘 GIU Units 51-52

remember / forget

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (1)

remember + -ing = memory of the past

remember + to = don't forget to do (future)

remember + -ing (past memory)
"I know I locked the door. I clearly remember locking it."
I did it, and now I remember this. 나는 그것을 했고, 지금 그것을 기억합니다.
remember + to (future task)
"I remembered to lock the door, but I forgot to shut the windows."
I remembered that I had to do it, so I did it. 해야 한다는 것을 기억하고, 그래서 했습니다.
"Remember to buy some bananas."
= Don't forget to buy them. = 바나나 사는 것을 잊지 마세요.

📘 GIU Unit 54, p.108-109

try / stop

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (2)

try + -ing = experiment

"Try pressing the green button."
Do it as an experiment or test. 실험 삼아 해보세요. (실험)

try + to = attempt (difficult)

"I was very tired. I tried to keep my eyes open, but I couldn't."
I attempted to do it, but it was difficult. 시도했지만 어려웠습니다. (시도)

stop + -ing = quit

"He stopped smoking."
He gave up the habit. 그 습관을 그만뒀습니다. (중단)

stop + to = pause in order to

"He stopped to smoke."
He paused what he was doing in order to smoke. 하던 일을 멈추고 담배를 피우기 위해. (목적)

📘 GIU Unit 55, p.110-111

regret

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (3)

regret + -ing = past regret

"I wish I hadn't done it."

regret + to = formal present

"I'm sorry to say..."

regret + -ing (past regret)
"I regret telling her the secret."
I told her, and now I wish I hadn't. 그녀에게 말했고, 지금 후회합니다.
regret + to (formal announcement)
"I regret to inform you that your application was unsuccessful."
Formal/official language: "I'm sorry to tell you..." 격식적 표현: "유감스럽게 알려드립니다..."

📘 GIU Unit 54, p.108-109

like / would like + -ing or to...

like / would like 뒤에 오는 -ing와 to부정사

Repeated actions: -ing or to... (no difference)

"Do you like getting up early?" = "Do you like to get up early?"
반복적인 행동에는 -ing와 to... 모두 가능 (의미 차이 없음)

Existing situation: -ing only

"Do you like being a student?" (not "to be")
이미 존재하는 상황에는 -ing만 사용

would like / would love + to...

"I'd like to go away for a few days."
"I wouldn't like to go on vacation alone."

would mind + -ing

"Would you mind closing the door, please?"
"I 'd like to have seen her again."
= I regret that I didn't see her. 보지 못한 것이 아쉽습니다.

📘 GIU Unit 56, p.112-113

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean does not distinguish between -ing and to after verbs. Students must memorize which verbs take which form.

한국어는 동사 뒤에 오는 -ing와 to를 구별하지 않습니다. 어떤 동사에 어떤 형태가 오는지 암기해야 합니다.

"I enjoy to play tennis."
enjoy is ALWAYS followed by -ing.
"I enjoy playing tennis."
"She avoided to speak in public."
avoid is ALWAYS followed by -ing.
"She avoided speaking in public."
avoid 뒤에는 항상 -ing가 옵니다.

📘 GIU Units 51-52

More Common Errors

추가적인 흔한 오류

Using -ing with "to" verbs
"I decided going to the party."
"I decided to go to the party."
decide is always followed by to + infinitive. decide 뒤에는 항상 to + 원형이 옵니다.
Confusing meaning-changing verbs
"Remember buying milk on your way home." (intended: don't forget)
"Remember to buy milk on your way home."
remember + to = don't forget (future action); remember + -ing = recall a past event. remember + to = 잊지 마세요 (미래 행동); remember + -ing = 과거 사건을 기억하다
