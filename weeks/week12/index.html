Verbs Followed by -ing

동명사(-ing)가 뒤따르는 동사

These verbs are ALWAYS followed by -ing

enjoy, mind, suggest, avoid, finish, practice, consider, imagine, deny, admit, risk, keep, give up
"I enjoy playing tennis."
"Would you mind closing the window?"
"She suggested going to the park."
"He avoided answering the question."

Verbs Followed by to...

to부정사가 뒤따르는 동사

These verbs are ALWAYS followed by to + infinitive

want, need, decide, hope, expect, plan, offer, agree, refuse, learn, promise, seem, pretend, afford, manage
"She decided to leave early."
"I hope to see you soon."
"He promised to help me."
"They agreed to meet at 6."

Verbs + -ing vs to... (1)

동명사 vs. to부정사 한눈에 보기 (1)

Verb + -ing

  • mind doing
  • suggest doing
  • avoid doing
  • finish doing

Verb + to...

  • want to do
  • need to do
  • decide to do
  • hope to do
  • expect to do

remember / forget

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (1)

remember + -ing = memory of the past

remember + to = don't forget to do (future)

remember + -ing (past memory)
"I remember locking the door."
I have a memory of doing it. (I locked it, and I remember this action.) 그것을 한 기억이 있습니다. (문을 잠갔고, 이 행동을 기억합니다.)
remember + to (future task)
"Remember to lock the door."
Don't forget to do it. (You haven't done it yet.) 잊지 말고 하세요. (아직 하지 않은 행동입니다.)

try / stop

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (2)

try + -ing = experiment

"Try pressing the button."
See what happens. 어떻게 되는지 해보세요. (실험)

try + to = attempt (difficult)

"I tried to open the door."
It was difficult; I attempted it. 어려웠지만 시도했습니다. (시도)

stop + -ing = quit

"He stopped smoking."
He gave up the habit. 그 습관을 그만뒀습니다. (중단)

stop + to = pause in order to

"He stopped to smoke."
He paused what he was doing in order to smoke. 하던 일을 멈추고 담배를 피우기 위해. (목적)

regret

-ing vs. to...로 의미가 달라지는 동사 (3)

regret + -ing = past regret

"I wish I hadn't done it."

regret + to = formal present

"I'm sorry to say..."

regret + -ing (past regret)
"I regret telling her the secret."
I told her, and now I wish I hadn't. 그녀에게 말했고, 지금 후회합니다.
regret + to (formal announcement)
"I regret to inform you that your application was unsuccessful."
Formal/official language: "I'm sorry to tell you..." 격식적 표현: "유감스럽게 알려드립니다..."

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자의 흔한 실수

L1 Interference Warning

Korean does not distinguish between -ing and to after verbs. Students must memorize which verbs take which form.

한국어는 동사 뒤에 오는 -ing와 to를 구별하지 않습니다. 어떤 동사에 어떤 형태가 오는지 암기해야 합니다.

"I enjoy to play tennis."
enjoy is ALWAYS followed by -ing.
"I enjoy playing tennis."
"She avoided to speak in public."
avoid is ALWAYS followed by -ing.
"She avoided speaking in public."
avoid 뒤에는 항상 -ing가 옵니다.

More Common Errors

추가적인 흔한 오류

Using -ing with "to" verbs
"I decided going to the party."
"I decided to go to the party."
decide is always followed by to + infinitive. decide 뒤에는 항상 to + 원형이 옵니다.
Confusing meaning-changing verbs
"Remember buying milk on your way home." (intended: don't forget)
"Remember to buy milk on your way home."
remember + to = don't forget (future action); remember + -ing = recall a past event. remember + to = 잊지 마세요 (미래 행동); remember + -ing = 과거 사건을 기억하다
