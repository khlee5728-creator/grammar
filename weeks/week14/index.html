Learning Objectives

  • Understand adjective + to... patterns 형용사 + to... 패턴을 이해한다
  • Master perception verbs: see/hear/watch + object + do/doing 지각동사: see/hear/watch + 목적어 + do/doing을 익힌다

  • Distinguish for + -ing (purpose of a thing) from to + verb (person's purpose) for + -ing (사물의 용도)와 to + 동사 (사람의 목적)를 구분한다

to... for Purpose / so that

목적을 나타내는 to... / so that

We use to + verb to say WHY somebody does something

Subject + verb ... + to + verb (= in order to / so as to)

Use so that (not to...) especially with can/could and will/would.

"so that"은 특히 can/could, will/would와 함께 사용합니다.

"I called the restaurant to make a reservation."
Purpose: to make a reservation. 목적: 예약하기 위해
"The president has a team of bodyguards to protect him."
Purpose: to protect him. 목적: 그를 보호하기 위해
"She's learning English so that she can study in Canada."
so that + can/could for purpose. so that + can/could로 목적을 표현

for + noun / for + -ing vs. to + verb

for + 명사 / for + -ing vs. to + 동사

Compare: for... and to...

for + noun  /  to + verb

Use for + -ing to say what something is USED for (purpose of a thing).
Do NOT use "for + -ing" to say why somebody does something — use "to + verb" instead.

for + -ing는 사물의 용도를 나타냅니다. 사람의 목적은 to + 동사를 사용하세요.

for + noun

"We stopped for gas."
for + noun for + 명사

to + verb

"We stopped to get gas."
to + verb to + 동사
"This brush is for washing the dishes."
for + -ing = what the thing is used for (purpose of the brush). for + -ing = 솔의 용도
"I went into the kitchen to wash the dishes."
to + verb = why the person did it. to + 동사 = 사람의 목적
"I went into the kitchen for washing the dishes."
WRONG: Do not use "for -ing" for a person's purpose. 틀림: 사람의 목적에는 for -ing를 쓰지 않음

📘 GIU Unit 62, p.124

Adjective + to...

형용사 + to 부정사

hard to understand, interesting to talk to, etc.

It's + adjective + to + verb  ↔  Subject + is + adjective + to + verb

Two ways to say the same thing: "It is hard to understand him." = "He is hard to understand."

같은 내용을 두 가지로 표현: "It is hard to understand him." = "He is hard to understand."

"He is hard to understand."
= It is hard to understand him. = 그를 이해하기 어렵다
"Do you think this water is safe to drink?"
= Do you think it is safe to drink this water? = 이 물을 마셔도 안전할까?
"The exam questions were impossible to answer."
= It was impossible to answer them. = 시험 문제에 답하는 것이 불가능했다
"Nicole is interesting to talk to."
= It's interesting to talk to her. Note the preposition "to" at the end. = 그녀와 이야기하는 것은 흥미롭다. 끝에 전치사 "to"에 주목.

📘 GIU Unit 63, p.126

nice of you to... / sorry to... / the first to...

nice of you to... / sorry to... / the first to...

More adjective + to... patterns

It's nice/kind/generous/stupid of somebody to... — comment on someone's action.
sorry to... / surprised to... — reaction to something.
the first/last/only/next to... — ordinal + to...
sure/likely/bound to... — probability.

It's nice of somebody to... = 누군가의 행동에 대한 평가
sorry to... = 반응 / the first to... = 순서 / bound to... = 가능성

"It was nice of you to take me to the airport."
nice of somebody to... = commenting on someone's action. nice of somebody to... = 행동에 대한 평가
"I'm sorry to hear that your mother isn't well."
sorry to... = expressing regret about something. sorry to... = 유감을 표현
"I was the only one to arrive on time."
the only/first/last + to + verb. the only/first/last + to + 동사
"She's bound to pass the exam."
bound to = certain to happen. bound to = 틀림없이 ~할 것이다

📘 GIU Unit 63, p.126-127

afraid to do vs. afraid of -ing

afraid to do vs. afraid of -ing / interested in vs. interested to

to... (afraid to do) and Preposition + -ing (afraid of -ing)

afraid to do = I don't want to do it (dangerous / bad result).
afraid of -ing = worried that something bad will happen.
interested in -ing = thinking of doing it.
interested to hear/see = it was interesting for me.

afraid to do = 위험해서 하고 싶지 않다 / afraid of -ing = 나쁜 일이 일어날까 걱정
interested in -ing = ~할 생각이다 / interested to hear = ~을 들으니 흥미로웠다

afraid to do / interested to

"People are afraid to walk here at night."
= they don't walk because it is dangerous. = 위험해서 걷지 않는다
"I was interested to hear that Tanya left her job."
= it was interesting for me. = 듣게 되어 흥미로웠다

afraid of -ing / interested in -ing

"We were afraid of falling."
= worried that we might fall (icy sidewalk). = 넘어질까 봐 걱정 (빙판길)
"Are you interested in joining the club?"
= thinking of joining. = 가입할 생각이 있나요?
"I'm sorry for shouting at you yesterday." vs. "I'm sorry to hear that Nicky lost her job."
sorry for -ing = apologize / sorry to... = regret about news. sorry for -ing = 사과 / sorry to... = 소식에 대한 유감

Perception Verbs: see/hear/watch + do/doing

지각동사: see/hear/watch + 원형/동명사

Two patterns with perception verbs

see/hear/watch/feel + object + base form (complete action)
see/hear/watch/feel + object + -ing (action in progress)

Base form = you saw the WHOLE action from start to finish. -ing = you saw the action IN PROGRESS.

동사원형 = 처음부터 끝까지 전체 행동을 봄. -ing = 진행 중인 행동을 봄.

"I saw Tom get into his car and drive away."
Complete action: he did it, and I saw the whole thing. 전체 행동: 차에 타서 떠나는 것을 처음부터 끝까지 봄
"Julia suddenly felt somebody touch her on the shoulder."
feel + object + base form (complete action). feel + 목적어 + 원형 (전체 행동)
"I didn't hear you come in."
= You came in, but I didn't hear it. = 들어왔지만 듣지 못했다

📘 GIU Unit 65, p.130

Perception Verbs: -ing (in progress)

지각동사: -ing (진행 중인 행동)

+ base form (complete)

동사원형 = 처음부터 끝까지

"I saw him do something."
= I saw the complete action from start to finish. 처음부터 끝까지 전체를 봄

+ -ing (in progress)

-ing = 진행 중인 일부만

"I saw him doing something."
= I saw him in the middle of doing it. 하고 있는 중간에 봄
"I saw Tom walking along the street."
= Tom was walking, and I saw him (in the middle of it). = Tom이 걸어가고 있었고, 나는 그것을 봤다 (중간에)
"I could hear it raining."
hear + -ing (in progress). hear + -ing (진행 중)
"Listen to the birds singing."
listen to + object + -ing (action in progress). listen to + 목적어 + -ing (진행 중인 행동)
"Can you smell something burning?"
smell + object + -ing (in progress). smell + 목적어 + -ing (진행 중)

📘 GIU Unit 65, p.130

-ing Phrases (Participle Clauses)

-ing구 (분사구문)

Two things happening at the same time / during another action

Subject + verb ... + -ing  (simultaneous)
Subject + verb ... + -ing  (= while ... was -ing)

동시 동작 또는 ~하는 동안의 동작을 -ing로 표현합니다.

"A man ran out of the house shouting."
= He ran out and was shouting (at the same time). = 뛰어나오면서 동시에 소리를 질렀다
"Joe hurt his knee playing football."
= while he was playing football. = 축구를 하는 동안
"Be careful crossing the road."
= Be careful when you cross the road. = 길을 건널 때 조심하세요
"Don't just stand there doing nothing!"
= standing and doing nothing at the same time. = 서서 아무것도 안 하고 있다

📘 GIU Unit 66, p.132

Having done... / -ing for reason

Having done... (먼저 완료된 행동) / -ing로 이유 표현

Earlier action: Having (done)...

When one action happens before another, use having + past participle for the first action.

하나의 행동이 다른 행동보다 먼저 일어날 때 having + 과거분사를 사용합니다.

"Having found a hotel, we looked for somewhere to eat."
= After we had found a hotel... = 호텔을 찾은 후에...
"Having finished her work, she went home."
= After finishing her work... = 일을 마친 후에...

Reason / Cause: -ing or Not -ing

Use -ing to explain something or say why somebody does something (= because...).

-ing를 이유/원인을 설명하는 데 사용합니다 (= because...).

"Feeling tired, I went to bed early."
= Because I felt tired... = 피곤했기 때문에...
"Not having a car, she finds it difficult to get around."
= Because she doesn't have a car... = 차가 없기 때문에...
"Not knowing what to do, I called my friend to ask her advice."
= Because I didn't know what to do... = 뭘 해야 할지 몰라서...

📘 GIU Unit 66, p.132

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ L1 Interference: Perception verbs do/doing distinction

Korean does not distinguish between "see someone do" and "see someone doing". Both translate the same way in Korean.

한국어는 "하는 것을 보다"로 동일하게 번역되어 구분이 어렵습니다.

"I saw him cross the road." = 전체 행동을 봄 (처음~끝)
"I saw him crossing the road." = 진행 중인 행동을 봄 (중간)
⚠ L1 Interference: Participle clauses feel unnatural

Korean uses different connective endings (-면서, -고, -아서) whereas English uses -ing phrases. Practice converting Korean connective patterns to English participle clauses.

한국어는 연결어미(-면서, -고, -아서)를 사용하지만 영어는 -ing구를 사용합니다.

"길을 걸으면서" → "Walking along the street, ..."
"뭘 해야 할지 몰라서" → "Not knowing what to do, ..."
