  • Distinguish for + -ing (purpose of a thing) from to + verb (person's purpose) for + -ing (사물의 용도)와 to + 동사 (사람의 목적)를 구분한다

to ..., for ..., and so that ... Unit 62A-D

목적을 나타내는 to... / so that

We use to + verb to say WHY somebody does something

📖 "We use to ... (to make ... / to change ..., etc.) to tell us the purpose of something." (Unit 62A)

Subject + verb ... + to + verb (= in order to / so as to)

Use so that (not to...) especially with can/could and will/would.

"so that"은 특히 can/could, will/would와 함께 사용합니다.

"I called the restaurant to make a reservation." — purpose
"The president has bodyguards to protect him." — purpose
"She's learning English so that she can study in Canada." — so that + can/could

📖 GIU Unit 62, p.124

for + noun / for + -ing vs. to + verb (1) Unit 62C

for + 명사 / for + -ing vs. to + 동사

Compare: for... and to...

for + noun / -ing  /  to + verb

for + -ing = purpose of a thing  |  to + verb = person's purpose

for + -ing = 사물의 용도 / to + 동사 = 사람의 목적

for + noun

"We stopped for gas."

to + verb

"We stopped to get gas."

📖 GIU Unit 62, p.124

for + -ing vs. to + verb (2) Unit 62C

for + -ing (사물의 용도) vs. to + verb (사람의 목적)

"This brush is for washing the dishes."
for + -ing = purpose of the brush. for + -ing = 솔의 용도
"I went into the kitchen to wash the dishes."
to + verb = why the person did it. to + 동사 = 사람의 목적
"I went into the kitchen for washing the dishes."
WRONG: Do not use "for -ing" for a person's purpose. 틀림: 사람의 목적에는 for -ing 불가

📖 GIU Unit 62, p.124

Adjective + to ... Unit 63A

형용사 + to 부정사

hard to understand, interesting to talk to, etc.

It's + adjective + to + verb  ↔  Subject + is + adjective + to + verb

"It is hard to understand him." = "He is hard to understand."

"He is hard to understand." — = It is hard to understand him.
"Is this water safe to drink?" — = Is it safe to drink this water?
"Nicole is interesting to talk to." — Note "to" at the end.

📖 GIU Unit 63, p.126

Adjective + to ... (nice of you / sorry to / bound to) Unit 63B-E

nice of you to... / sorry to... / the first to...

More adjective + to... patterns

nice/kind/stupid of sb to... = comment  |  sorry to... = reaction  |  the first/last to... = ordinal  |  bound to... = probability

"It was nice of you to take me to the airport."
nice of somebody to... 행동에 대한 평가
"I'm sorry to hear that your mother isn't well." — sorry to = regret
"She's bound to pass the exam." — bound to = certain to happen

📖 GIU Unit 63, p.126-127

to ... (afraid to do) and Preposition + -ing (afraid of -ing) Unit 64A

afraid to do vs. afraid of -ing

to... (afraid to do) and Preposition + -ing (afraid of -ing)

📖 "I am afraid to do something = I don't want to do it because it is dangerous or the result could be bad." (Unit 64A)

afraid to do = don't want to (dangerous)  |  afraid of -ing = worried something bad will happen

afraid to do = 위험해서 하고 싶지 않다 / afraid of -ing = 나쁜 일이 일어날까 걱정

afraid to do

"People are afraid to walk here at night."

afraid of -ing

"We were afraid of falling."

📖 GIU Unit 64, p.128

to ... and Preposition + -ing (interested / sorry) Unit 64B-C

interested in vs. interested to / sorry for vs. sorry to

interested in -ing = thinking of doing it  |  interested to hear = it was interesting for me
sorry for -ing = apologize  |  sorry to... = regret about news

interested in -ing = ~할 생각 / interested to hear = 흥미로웠다 / sorry for -ing = 사과 / sorry to... = 유감

"I was interested to hear that Tanya left her job."
= it was interesting for me. = 듣게 되어 흥미로웠다
"Are you interested in joining the club?"
= thinking of joining. = 가입할 생각이 있나요?
"I'm sorry for shouting at you yesterday."
sorry for -ing = apologize sorry for -ing = 사과

📖 GIU Unit 64, p.128

see somebody do and see somebody doing Unit 65

지각동사: see/hear/watch + 원형/동명사

Two patterns with perception verbs

see/hear/watch/feel + object + base form (complete action)
see/hear/watch/feel + object + -ing (action in progress)
"I saw Tom get into his car and drive away." — complete action
"I didn't hear you come in." — complete action (didn't hear)

📖 GIU Unit 65, p.130

see somebody doing (in progress) Unit 65B-C

지각동사: -ing (진행 중인 행동)

+ base form (complete)

"I saw him do something."
= complete action 전체를 봄

+ -ing (in progress)

"I saw him doing something."
= in the middle of it 중간에 봄
"I saw Tom walking along the street."  |  "I could hear it raining."
-ing = action in progress when perceived. -ing = 지각 시점에 진행 중인 행동

📖 GIU Unit 65, p.130

-ing Phrases (He hurt his knee playing football.) Unit 66A

-ing구 (분사구문)

Two things happening at the same time / during another action

Subject + verb ... + -ing  (simultaneous / = while ... was -ing)
"A man ran out of the house shouting." — simultaneous actions
"Joe hurt his knee playing football." — = while playing
"Be careful crossing the road." — = when you cross

📖 GIU Unit 66, p.132

-ing Phrases — Having done / Feeling tired Unit 66B-C

Having done... (먼저 완료된 행동)

Earlier action: Having + past participle

Use having + p.p. when one action happens before another.

먼저 일어난 행동에 having + 과거분사를 사용합니다.

"Having found a hotel, we looked for somewhere to eat."
"Having finished her work, she went home."

Reason / Cause: -ing or Not -ing (= because...)

-ing로 이유를 설명합니다 (= because...).

"Feeling tired, I went to bed early."  |  "Not knowing what to do, I called my friend."

📖 GIU Unit 66, p.132

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

⚠ L1 Interference: Perception verbs do/doing distinction

Korean does not distinguish "see someone do" and "see someone doing".

"I saw him cross the road." = 전체 행동  |  "I saw him crossing the road." = 진행 중
⚠ L1 Interference: Participle clauses feel unnatural

Korean uses connective endings (-면서, -고, -아서); English uses -ing phrases.

"걸으면서" → "Walking along the street, ..."  |  "몰라서" → "Not knowing what to do, ..."
