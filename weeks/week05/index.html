Learning Objectives

이번 주 학습 목표

  • Use present tenses (continuous & simple) for future events 미래 사건에 현재 시제를 사용한다

Present Continuous for Future

미래를 나타내는 현재진행형

📖 GIU Unit 18, p.36-37

Fixed Arrangements

Use the present continuous for definite arrangements in the future (often with other people).

미래의 확정된 약속/일정에 현재진행형을 사용합니다 (주로 다른 사람과 함께).

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + future time
"He is playing tennis on Monday afternoon."
Ben's diary — already arranged. 벤의 일정 — 이미 약속이 잡힘.
"I'm not working tomorrow, so we can go out somewhere."
Planned arrangement. 계획된 일정.
"What time is Katherine arriving tomorrow?"
Asking about a fixed arrangement. 확정된 약속에 대해 묻기.

Present Simple for Future

미래를 나타내는 현재 단순형

📖 GIU Unit 18B, p.36-37

Timetables & Schedules

Use the present simple for timetables, schedules, and fixed events.

시간표, 일정, 고정된 행사에 현재 단순형을 사용합니다.

"My flight leaves at 11:30."
Fixed timetable. 고정 시간표
"What time does the movie start tonight?"
Official schedule. 공식 일정
Compare: 비교:
"What time are you arriving?" (personal arrangement)
"What time does the train arrive?" (timetable)

(be) going to (1)

going to: 의도 & 계획

📖 GIU Unit 19, p.38-39

Subject + am/is/are going to + base form

1. Intentions / Plans (decided before)

"She's going to buy a new car." (Already decided.)
Decision made before speaking. 이미 결정했음.

2. Predictions based on evidence

"Look at those black clouds! It's going to rain."
I can see the evidence now. 지금 증거를 볼 수 있음.

3. was/were going to (changed plans)

"We were going to take the train, but decided to drive."

Will / Shall (1)

will: 즉석 결정

📖 GIU Unit 20, p.40-41

Subject + will + base form

1. Decisions at the moment of speaking

"Oh, I left the door open. I'll go and shut it."
Deciding now. 지금 결정하는 것.

2. Offering & Promising

"That bag looks heavy. I'll help you with it."
Spontaneous offer. 자발적 제안.
"I won't tell anyone. I promise."
Making a promise. 약속하기.

Will / Shall (2)

will: 거부 (won't)

📖 GIU Unit 20, p.40-41

3. Refusal (won't)

"The car won't start."
"won't" = refuses to (for things too). "won't" = 거부 (사물에도 사용).
"I've tried to give her advice, but she won't listen."
She refuses to listen. 들으려고 하지 않는다.

Will for Predictions

will: 예측 (의견/믿음)

📖 GIU Unit 21, p.42-43

Predicting the future with will

Use will for things we believe or expect to happen in the future.

미래에 일어날 것이라고 믿거나 예상하는 일에 will을 사용합니다.

"Kate will pass the driving test. She's a good driver."
Prediction based on belief. 믿음에 기반한 예측.

Common patterns with will

"I'll probably be home late tonight." / "I'm sure you'll pass."
"I don't think the exam will be very difficult."
Note: "I don't think it will..." (not "I think it won't..."). "I don't think it will..."이 자연스러운 표현.
⚠ After "I hope" → use present (not will): "I hope" 뒤에는 현재형:
"I hope Kate passes the driving test." (not will pass)

Will vs Going to (1)

will과 going to 비교

📖 GIU Unit 22, p.44-45

will — new decision

Decision made now.

"Let's have a party. We'll invite lots of people."
Just decided. 방금 결정.

going to — already decided

Decision made before.

"We're going to invite lots of people."
Already decided earlier. 이미 결정.
Predictions — both OK. 예측에는 둘 다 가능. But with evidence → use going to: "Look at those black clouds. It's going to rain." (not it will rain)

Future Continuous

미래 진행형

📖 GIU Unit 23, p.46-47

Subject + will be + verb-ing

Use for an action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

미래의 특정 시점에 진행 중일 행동을 나타냅니다.

"This time tomorrow, I'll be flying to Paris."
In progress at that specific future moment. 미래의 그 특정 시점에 진행 중.
"Don't call me at 8. I'll be having dinner."
The activity will be ongoing at that time. 그 시간에 활동이 진행 중일 것.

Future Perfect

미래 완료

📖 GIU Unit 23D, p.46-47

Subject + will have + past participle (V3)

Use for something that will be completed before a specific future time.

미래의 특정 시점 이전에 완료될 일을 나타냅니다.

"By next year, I'll have graduated."
Graduation will be complete before next year. 내년 이전에 졸업이 완료될 것.
"By the time you arrive, I'll have cooked dinner."
Cooking will be finished before your arrival. 네가 도착하기 전에 요리가 끝날 것.

Future Tenses on the Timeline

타임라인으로 보는 미래 시제

Future simple (will do): a point in the future

Future continuous (will be doing): in progress at a future time

Future perfect (will have done): completed before a future time

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

📖 GIU Units 18-22

⚠ Overusing "will" for all future expressions

Korean has one main future marker (~할 것이다), but English has multiple ways to express the future. Don't always default to "will"!

한국어는 "~할 것이다"로 미래를 표현하지만, 영어는 상황에 따라 다른 표현을 사용합니다.

"I will meet John at 7 tomorrow." (when it's already arranged)
"I'm meeting John at 7 tomorrow."
Already arranged → present continuous. 이미 약속이 잡혔음 → 현재진행형
"I will buy a new laptop." (when you decided earlier)
"I'm going to buy a new laptop."
Decided before this moment → going to. 이미 결정한 것 → going to

Week 5 Summary

5주차 요약

  • Present continuous: fixed arrangements ("I'm meeting...") 현재진행형: 확정된 약속
  • Present simple: timetables and schedules 현재 단순형: 시간표와 일정
  • going to: intentions (decided before) + evidence-based predictions going to: 의도 + 증거 기반 예측
  • will: instant decisions, opinions, offers, promises will: 즉석 결정, 의견, 제안, 약속
  • Future continuous (will be + V-ing): in progress at a future time 미래진행형: 미래 시점에 진행 중
  • Future perfect (will have + V3): completed before a future time 미래완료: 미래 시점 이전에 완료
