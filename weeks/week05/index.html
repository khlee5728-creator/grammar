I am doing / I do (Present Tenses with a Future Meaning) Unit 18A

미래를 나타내는 현재진행형

Fixed Arrangements

Use the present continuous for definite arrangements in the future (often with other people).

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + future time

📖 "I'm doing something (tomorrow) = I have already decided and arranged to do it." (Unit 18A)

"He is playing tennis on Monday afternoon."
Ben's diary — already arranged. 벤의 일정 — 이미 약속이 잡힘.
"I'm not working tomorrow, so we can go out somewhere."
Planned arrangement. 계획된 일정.

📖 GIU Unit 18A, p.36-37

I am doing / I do (Present Tenses with a Future Meaning) Unit 18B

미래를 나타내는 현재 단순형

Timetables & Schedules

Use the present simple for timetables, schedules, and fixed events.

📖 "We use the simple present when we talk about schedules and programs." (Unit 18B)

"My flight leaves at 11:30."
"What time does the movie start tonight?"
Compare:
"What time are you arriving?" (personal arrangement)
"What time does the train arrive?" (timetable)

📖 GIU Unit 18B, p.36-37

I'm going to (do) (Future Intentions) Unit 19A-C

going to: 의도 & 계획

Subject + am/is/are going to + base form

📖 "I am going to do something = I have already decided to do it." (Unit 19A)

1. Intentions / Plans (decided before)

"She's going to buy a new car." (Already decided.)

2. Predictions based on evidence

"Look at those black clouds! It's going to rain."

3. was/were going to (changed plans)

"We were going to take the train, but decided to drive."

📖 GIU Unit 19A-D, p.38-39

will 1 (Decisions and Offers) Unit 20A-B

will: 즉석 결정

Subject + will + base form

📖 "We use I'll when we've just decided to do something." (Unit 20A)

1. Decisions at the moment of speaking

"Oh, I left the door open. I'll go and shut it."

2. Offering & Promising

"That bag looks heavy. I'll help you with it."
"I won't tell anyone. I promise."

📖 GIU Unit 20A-B, p.40-41

will 1 (Refusal with won't) Unit 20B

will: 거부 (won't)

📖 "We use won't to say that somebody refuses to do something." (Unit 20B)

3. Refusal (won't)

"The car won't start."
"won't" = refuses to (for things too). "won't" = 거부 (사물에도 사용).
"I've tried to give her advice, but she won't listen."
She refuses to listen. 들으려고 하지 않는다.

📖 GIU Unit 20B-C, p.40-41

will 2 (Predictions) Unit 21A-B

will: 예측

Use will for things we believe or expect to happen.

📖 "We use will to say what we know or believe about the future." (Unit 21A)

"Kate will pass the driving test. She's a good driver."

Common patterns with will

"I'll probably be home late tonight." / "I'm sure you'll pass."
"I don't think the exam will be very difficult."
⚠ After "I hope" → use present (not will):
"I hope Kate passes the driving test." (not will pass)

📖 GIU Unit 21A-B, p.42-43

Will vs Going to (1)

will과 going to 비교

📖 GIU Unit 22, p.44-45

will — new decision

Decision made now.

"Let's have a party. We'll invite lots of people."
Just decided. 방금 결정.

going to — already decided

Decision made before.

"We're going to invite lots of people."
Already decided earlier. 이미 결정.
Predictions — both OK. 예측에는 둘 다 가능. But with evidence → use going to: "Look at those black clouds. It's going to rain." (not it will rain)

Future Tenses on the Timeline

타임라인으로 보는 미래 시제

Present continuous (I'm doing): fixed arrangements 확정된 약속

Present simple (I do): timetables & schedules 시간표/일정

going to: intentions & evidence-based predictions 의도/증거 기반 예측

will: decisions, predictions, offers, promises 결정/예측/제안/약속

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

📖 GIU Units 18-22

⚠ Overusing "will" for all future expressions

Korean uses one future marker (~할 것이다), but English has multiple forms. Don't default to "will"!

"I will meet John at 7 tomorrow." (when it's already arranged)
"I'm meeting John at 7 tomorrow."
Already arranged → present continuous. 이미 약속이 잡혔음 → 현재진행형
"I will buy a new laptop." (when you decided earlier)
"I'm going to buy a new laptop."
Decided before this moment → going to. 이미 결정한 것 → going to
