Present Continuous for Future Unit 18

미래를 나타내는 현재진행형 — 확정된 약속

A Definite arrangements in the future

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + future time

Use the present continuous for definite arrangements in the future (often with other people).

미래에 이미 확정된 약속/일정에는 현재진행형을 사용합니다.

📖 "I'm doing something (tomorrow) = I have already decided and arranged to do it." (Unit 18A)

"He is playing tennis on Monday afternoon."
Already arranged. 이미 약속이 잡힘.
"I'm not working tomorrow, so we can go out somewhere."
Planned arrangement. 계획된 일정.

📘 GIU Unit 18A, p.36-37

Present Simple for Future Unit 18

미래를 나타내는 현재 단순형 — 시간표/일정

B Timetables, schedules & fixed events

Use the present simple for timetables, schedules, and fixed events.

시간표, 일정, 고정된 이벤트에는 현재 단순형을 사용합니다.

📖 "We use the simple present when we talk about schedules and programs." (Unit 18B)

"My flight leaves at 11:30."
"What time does the movie start tonight?"
Compare A vs B:
"What time are you arriving?" (personal arrangement → A)
"What time does the train arrive?" (timetable → B)

📘 GIU Unit 18B, p.36-37

I'm going to (do) Unit 19

going to 의 네 가지 용법

Subject + am/is/are going to + base form

A Intentions & Plans (decided before)

"She's going to buy a new car." 이미 결정한 계획

B I am doing vs I am going to do

"I'm leaving next week." 확정된 약속
"I'm going to polish them." 결정만 한 상태

Often the difference is small & either form is possible. 차이가 작으면 둘 다 가능

C Predictions based on evidence

"Look at those black clouds! It's going to rain." 눈에 보이는 증거

D was/were going to (changed plans)

"We were going to take the train, but decided to drive." 바뀐 계획

📘 GIU Unit 19A-D, p.38-39

will 1 — Instant Decisions Unit 20

will: 말하는 순간의 결정

A Deciding at the moment of speaking

Subject + will + base form

Use will when you decide to do something at the moment of speaking.

말하는 순간에 결정할 때 will을 사용합니다.

📖 "We use I'll when we've just decided to do something." (Unit 20A)

"Oh, I left the door open. I'll go and shut it."
Just decided now. 방금 결정함.
"I'll have the chicken, please."
Deciding at the moment (e.g. in a restaurant). 그 순간에 결정 (예: 레스토랑).

📘 GIU Unit 20A, p.40-41

will 1 — Offers, Promises & More Unit 20

will: 제안, 약속, 거부, 비교

B Offering, Promising & Refusal

"That bag looks heavy. I'll help you." 제안
"I won't tell anyone. I promise." 약속
"The car won't start." won't = 거부 (사물에도 사용)

C Don't use will for already decided/arranged

"I'll go on vacation next Saturday."
"I'm going on vacation next Saturday."

Use will for instant decisions, not for plans already decided. 이미 결정된 계획에는 will ❌

📘 GIU Unit 20B-C, p.40-41

will 2 — Predictions Unit 21

will: 예측과 확신

A Predictions (believe / expect)

Use will for things we believe or expect to happen.

일어날 것이라고 믿거나 예상할 때 will을 사용합니다.

"Kate will pass the driving test. She's a good driver."

B Common patterns with will

"I'll probably be home late." / "I'm sure you'll pass."
"I don't think the exam will be very difficult."

C will for talking about now

"Don't call Amy now. She'll be busy." 지금 바쁠 거야 (현재 추측)
⚠ After "I hope" → use present (not will):
"I hope Kate passes the driving test." (not will pass)

📘 GIU Unit 21A-C, p.42-43

I will and I'm going to Unit 22

will과 going to 비교

A Future actions: will vs going to

will — new decision

Decision made now.

"We'll invite lots of people."
Just decided. 방금 결정.

going to — already decided

Decision made before.

"We're going to invite lots of people."
Already decided. 이미 결정.

B Predictions: will and going to

Both OK for predictions. 예측에는 둘 다 가능.

But with evidence → use going to: "Look at those black clouds. It's going to rain." (not it will rain)

📘 GIU Unit 22A-B, p.44-45

Choosing the Right Future Form

상황별 미래 표현 비교

상황 Situation 표현 Form 예문 Example
확정된 약속/배정
Fixed arrangement		 I'm doing
(present continuous)		 "I'm meeting Tom tomorrow."
시간표/일정
Timetable / schedule		 I do
(present simple)		 "The train leaves at 9:00."
의도/결정한 계획
Intention / decided plan		 going to "I'm going to buy a new car."
증거 기반 예측
Prediction (evidence)		 going to "Look! It's going to rain."
즉석 결정
Instant decision		 will "I'll help you with that."
예측/제안/약속
Prediction / offer / promise		 will "I'm sure she'll pass the exam."

Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

한국어 화자가 자주 하는 실수

📖 GIU Units 18-22

⚠ Overusing "will" for all future expressions

Korean uses one future marker (~할 것이다), but English has multiple forms. Don't default to "will"!

"I will meet John at 7 tomorrow." (when it's already arranged)
"I'm meeting John at 7 tomorrow."
Already arranged → present continuous. 이미 약속이 잡혔음 → 현재진행형
"I will buy a new laptop." (when you decided earlier)
"I'm going to buy a new laptop."
Decided before this moment → going to. 이미 결정한 것 → going to

Week 5 Summary

5주차 요약

  • Present continuous: fixed arrangements ("I'm meeting...") 현재진행형: 확정된 약속
  • Present simple: timetables and schedules 현재 단순형: 시간표와 일정
  • going to: intentions (decided before) + evidence-based predictions going to: 의도 + 증거 기반 예측
  • will: instant decisions, opinions, offers, promises will: 즉석 결정, 의견, 제안, 약속
