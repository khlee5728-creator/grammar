WEEK 02

Present & Past

현재와 과거 시제

Units 1 – 6

Welcome to Week 2. This week we cover the present continuous, simple present, simple past, and past continuous tenses. Students should already be familiar with basic sentence structure from Week 1.

Learning Objectives

학습 목표

  • Distinguish present continuous from simple present
    현재진행형과 단순현재를 구별할 수 있다
  • Understand state verbs vs action verbs
    상태동사와 동작동사의 차이를 이해할 수 있다
  • Use simple past correctly for completed actions
    완료된 동작에 단순과거를 올바르게 사용할 수 있다
  • Master past continuous for interrupted actions
    중단된 동작에 과거진행형을 사용할 수 있다
Review these four objectives with students. Emphasize the Korean interference issues especially for state verbs, which is a very common mistake area.

Present Continuous

현재진행형 (Unit 1)

Structure

Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

When do we use it?

  • Actions happening right now 지금 일어나고 있는 동작
  • Temporary situations 일시적인 상황
  • Changing / developing situations 변화하고 있는 상황
Write the formula on the board. Point out that the be-verb must agree with the subject: I am, he/she/it is, you/we/they are. Practice the -ing spelling rules: drop silent e (make → making), double consonant (run → running).

Present Continuous: Examples

현재진행형 예문

The water is boiling. Can you turn it off?
Something happening right now → present continuous
지금 일어나고 있는 일 → 현재진행형
The water boils. Can you turn it off?
Simple present is wrong here — the water is boiling right now, not as a general fact.
지금 끓고 있는 것이므로 단순현재가 아니라 현재진행형을 써야 합니다.
Look! Somebody is climbing that tree over there.
"Look!" signals something happening right now.
"Look!"은 지금 일어나고 있는 일을 가리킵니다.
Ask students: "What is happening in this classroom RIGHT NOW?" to elicit present continuous sentences. Encourage them to look around and describe what they see.

Simple Present

단순현재 (Unit 2)

Structure

Subject + base form (+ s / es for 3rd person singular)

When do we use it?

  • Habits and routines 습관과 일과
  • General truths and facts 일반적 진실과 사실
  • Permanent situations 영구적인 상황
Remind students about the third-person singular rules: add -s (works), add -es (watches, goes), change y to -ies (studies). These spelling rules are easy to forget.

Simple Present: Examples

단순현재 예문

I always go to work by car.
Habitual action → simple present
습관적인 동작 → 단순현재
I 'm always going to work by car.
Present continuous with "always" implies annoyance, not a regular habit.
"always"와 현재진행형을 쓰면 짜증을 표현하는 것이지, 단순한 습관이 아닙니다.
Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
Scientific fact / general truth → simple present
과학적 사실 → 단순현재
She studies English every morning.
Regular habit, 3rd person singular → add -ies
규칙적 습관, 3인칭 단수 → -ies 추가
Contrast "I always go" (habit, neutral) with "I'm always going" (complaining). This is a subtle distinction. Ask students: can "always + present continuous" express a positive feeling? (Yes, sometimes, e.g., "She's always helping people.")

Common Mistake: State Verbs

한국인 학습자 주의점: 상태동사

L1 Interference Alert / 모국어 간섭 주의

한국어에서는 '알고 있다', '믿고 있다'처럼 진행형으로 표현하지만, 영어에서는 상태동사는 진행형을 쓰지 않습니다.

I am knowing the answer.

I know the answer.

I am believing you.

I believe you.

State verbs (no continuous form):

know like want need prefer believe understand belong mean remember depend suppose

This is one of the most common errors for Korean speakers. In Korean, 알고 있다 (know), 믿고 있다 (believe) use a progressive-like form. Drill students on these state verbs. You might want to create flashcards for this list.

Present Continuous vs Simple Present

현재진행형 vs 단순현재 비교 (Unit 3)

Present Continuous

  • Happening now
    The water is boiling.
    지금 일어나고 있는 일
  • Temporary
    I'm living with a friend until I find an apartment.
    일시적인 상황
  • Changing situations
    Your English is getting better.
    변화하고 있는 상황

Simple Present

  • Always / usually
    Water boils at 100°C.
    항상 / 보통 일어나는 일
  • Permanent
    I live in Seoul.
    영구적인 상황
  • Facts / general truths
    The sun rises in the east.
    사실 / 일반적 진실
Use this comparison slide to consolidate understanding. Ask students to generate their own pairs. Example prompt: "Tell me something happening right now AND something that is always true."

Present Tenses: Timeline

현재 시제 타임라인

Present Continuous (happening now) Simple Present (always true)
Use the timeline to show how present continuous focuses on the moment of speaking while simple present spans across all time as a general truth.

Simple Past

단순과거 (Unit 4)

Structure

Subject + past form (verb-ed / irregular)

When do we use it?

  • Completed actions in the past 과거에 완료된 동작
  • A specific time in the past 과거의 특정 시점
  • Sequence of past events 과거 사건의 나열
Review regular past tense formation: add -ed (worked), add -d (lived), double consonant + ed (stopped), change y to -ied (studied). Then introduce the concept of irregular verbs (go→went, see→saw, take→took). Suggest students start memorizing the irregular verb list.

Simple Past: Examples

단순과거 예문

I washed my car yesterday. Then I drove to the beach.
Two completed past actions in sequence → simple past for both
과거에 완료된 연속 동작 → 둘 다 단순과거
Mozart wrote more than 600 pieces of music.
A historical fact about a person who is no longer alive → simple past
이미 세상을 떠난 사람에 대한 역사적 사실 → 단순과거
We arrived at the hotel at 8:00 last night.
A specific time in the past → simple past
과거의 특정 시점 → 단순과거
Emphasize that simple past always implies the action is DONE and FINISHED. Time markers like yesterday, last night, in 2020, ago are strong clues for simple past.

Past Continuous

과거진행형 (Unit 5)

Structure

Subject + was / were + verb-ing

When do we use it?

  • Action in progress at a specific past time 과거 특정 시점에 진행 중이던 동작
  • Interrupted actions (with "when") "when"과 함께 중단된 동작
  • Two actions happening at the same time 동시에 일어난 두 동작
Draw attention to the parallel with present continuous: same structure (be + -ing) but the be-verb is in the past (was/were). This pattern helps students see the systematic nature of English tenses.

Past Continuous: Examples

과거진행형 예문

I was walking home when it started to rain.
Longer action (walking) interrupted by shorter action (started) → past continuous + simple past
긴 동작(걷고 있었다)이 짧은 동작(비가 시작됐다)에 의해 중단 → 과거진행 + 단순과거
What were you doing at 10:00 last night?
Asking about an action in progress at a specific past time
과거 특정 시점에 진행 중이던 동작을 묻는 질문
While I was cooking, my brother was setting the table.
Two simultaneous past actions → past continuous for both
동시에 일어난 두 동작 → 둘 다 과거진행형
Draw a timeline on the board: a long arrow for the continuous action and an X on it for the interrupting event. Ask students to create their own "when" sentences using this pattern.

Simple Past vs Past Continuous

단순과거 vs 과거진행형 비교 (Unit 6)

Simple Past

  • Completed action
    I walked to school.
    완료된 동작
  • Short / interrupting action
    The phone rang.
    짧은 / 중단시킨 동작
  • Sequence of events
    She got up and made coffee.
    연속적으로 일어난 사건

Past Continuous

  • Action in progress
    I was walking to school (when...).
    진행 중이던 동작
  • Long / interrupted action
    I was sleeping when the phone rang.
    긴 / 중단된 동작
  • Simultaneous actions
    She was reading while he was cooking.
    동시에 일어난 동작
This is a key contrast slide. Make sure students understand the "interrupted action" pattern: past continuous for the background action, simple past for the interrupting event. Use gestures: hold one hand moving (continuous) and chop with the other hand (interruption).

Past Tenses: Timeline

과거 시제 타임라인

Simple Past (completed) Past Continuous (in progress, then interrupted)
Use the timeline to illustrate the difference visually. The simple past is a single point (a completed event) while the past continuous is a range that gets interrupted.

연습 퀴즈 — Units 1–6

Give students time to answer each question individually before reviewing. Encourage them to think about WHY each answer is correct, not just WHAT the correct answer is. Discuss any questions that many students get wrong.

Key Takeaways

핵심 정리

  • Present continuous = happening now, temporary, changing
    현재진행형 = 지금, 일시적, 변화 중
  • Simple present = habits, facts, permanent situations
    단순현재 = 습관, 사실, 영구적 상황
  • State verbs (know, believe, want...) do NOT use continuous forms
    상태동사 (know, believe, want...)는 진행형을 쓰지 않음
  • Simple past = completed past actions at a specific time
    단순과거 = 과거에 완료된 동작
  • Past continuous = action in progress when another event interrupted
    과거진행형 = 다른 사건에 의해 중단된 진행 중이던 동작
  • Pattern: I was doing X when Y happened.
    패턴: ~하고 있었는데 그때 ~가 일어났다
Review these six points quickly. Ask students to hold up fingers 1-6 for any point they still find confusing. Address the most commonly raised points before moving on.
